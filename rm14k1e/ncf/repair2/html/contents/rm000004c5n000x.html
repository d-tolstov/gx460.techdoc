ADAPTIVE VARIABLE SUSPENSION SYSTEM > GENERAL



OUTLINE

  1. An Adaptive Variable Suspension System (AVS) is used.

  2. The AVS controls the damping force of the shock absorbers for all 4 wheels in accordance with the driving conditions and road surface conditions. In this way, it achieves a comfortable ride, excellent driveability and high levels of stability.

  3. The AVS is a semi-active type of suspension. Its suspension control ECU estimates the vehicle conditions based on the signals from the sensors and the absorber control switch. Then, the suspension control ECU actuates the absorber control actuator, varies the flow of the oil, and controls the damping force.

  4. By operating the absorber control switch, the driver can select 3 types of damping forces for the shock absorbers.

  5. Non-linear H∞ control is used for the basic control of the damping force.

    HINT:
    1. To summarize, H∞ control is a theory for designing a controller that meets the control specifications that are represented by the H∞ norm (a measurement unit of the transfer function in the system). When this is expanded into a non-linear system, it is called "non-linear H∞ control".
    2. The "H" is the initial letter of the mathematician named Hardy (who studied the stability of control systems) who advocated the mathematical space that is handled by this control logic. The "∞" represents the ∞ norm, which is one of the mathematical units used for measuring the size of the signals.
