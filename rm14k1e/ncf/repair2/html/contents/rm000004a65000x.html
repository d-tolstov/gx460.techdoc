FUNCTION OF MAIN COMPONENTS

  1. G-BOOK System

    Component
    		Function
    Telephone Switch Assembly
    		When the telephone switch assembly is pressed, a switch signal is sent to the telematics transceiver. (When a manual maintenance check or manual emergency call is performed.)
    LED Indicator Light*
    		Green
    1. Turns on when the system is under a service contract and operating normally within the service area.
    2. Blinks during a call or a maintenance check and turns off when the system is malfunctioning.
    3. Blinks when in diagnostic mode to indicate a Diagnostic Trouble Code (DTC).
    Red
    1. Turns on when the vehicle is not within the service area.
    2. Blinks when a call is made while the system is still malfunctioning.
    3. Turns off when the system is operating normally.
    4. Blinks when the back-up battery needs to be replaced.
    5. Blinks when in diagnostic mode to indicate a DTC.
    Telephone Microphone Assembly
    1. When the hands-free function is used: Sends the microphone voice signal to the display and navigation module display.
    2. When an emergency call is made: Sends the microphone voice signal to the telematics transceiver.
    Telephone Antenna Assembly
    		Sends and receives the data and voice signals used for the G-BOOK service through a communication network.
    Telematics Transceiver (Data Communication Module)
    1. Uses the telephone antenna assembly to send and receive the data and voice signals used for the G-BOOK service through a communication network.
      1. When the operator service is used: Sends a received voice signal to the display and navigation module display.
      2. When the operator service is used: Sends a sent voice signal the display and navigation module display, and to the telephone antenna assembly.
      3. When an emergency call is made: Sends a received voice signal to the vehicle speakers.
      4. When an emergency call is made: Receives a sent voice signal from the telephone microphone assembly.
      5. When an emergency call is made: Sends a mute signal to the stereo component amplifier assembly. (Mute function becomes active during the call.)
      6. When an emergency call is made: Sends the location information to the G-BOOK center.
    Back-up Battery (BUB)
    1. The back-up battery is a lithium manganese dioxide non-rechargeable battery.
    2. Checks the battery condition and sends the self-diagnosis result to the display and navigation module display via the telematics transceiver (Data Communication Module).
    3. When the telematics transceiver (Data Communication Module) receives a Diagnostic Trouble Code (DTC) indicating that the back-up battery is "malfunctioning", "deteriorated" or "not compatible", the telematics transceiver blinks the red LED indicator light to inform the driver. The telematics transceiver will also store a DTC.
    Navigation Antenna Assembly
    1. Receives GPS radio waves and sends them to the display and navigation module display.
    2. The vehicle location is transmitted together with the emergency call.
    Main Body ECU
    (Driver Side Junction Block Assembly)
    		Sends a security horn sounding signal to the display and navigation module display when the theft deterrent system is activated.
    Display and Navigation Module Display
    1. The display and navigation module display receives an image signal and displays the image on its screen.
    2. Sends and receives the data used for the G-BOOK service to and from the telematics transceiver using a telematics data signal.
      1. When the operator service is used: Sends the sent voice signal from the telephone microphone assembly to the telematics transceiver.
      2. When the operator service is used: Sends the received voice signal from the telematics transceiver to the stereo component amplifier assembly.
      3. Periodically sends the vehicle location information to the telematics transceiver.
      4. When a warning comes on, the display and navigation module display receives a warning on signal from the combination meter assembly and the telematics transceiver sends it to the G-BOOK center.
      5. When the theft deterrent system is activated, the telematics transceiver sends the G-BOOK center that the security horn has sounded.
    Stereo Component Amplifier Assembly
    		Receives the received voice signal sent from the display and navigation module display and outputs it as sound from the vehicle speakers.
    (When the hands-free function is used.)
    Center Airbag Sensor Assembly
    		Sends an activation signal to the telematics transceiver when the airbags deploy.
    (An automatic emergency call is made.)

    HINT:
    *: The green and red indicator lights turn on for 5 seconds immediately after the engine switch is turned on (ACC).


FUNCTION


  1. Emergency Call Service

    1. The emergency call service automatically makes a call to a G-BOOK center operator when airbags deploy in the event of an accident, in order to support prompt attendance by emergency personnel.



    2. In the event of trouble such as an accident or medical emergency, the telematics transceiver can be used to send the emergency call center the voice signal, location information and the registered user information.



    3. The emergency call service can be used either manually or automatically to contact the G-BOOK center.

      Call Method
      		Operation Condition
      Automatic Call
      		An automatic call is made if the airbags have deployed. The call occurs regardless of the condition of the occupants.
      Manual Call
      		In the event of a medical emergency or an accident that does not cause the airbags to deploy, a manual call can be made by pressing the telephone switch assembly in the map light assembly.
      (To press the telephone switch assembly, the telephone switch assembly cover needs to be removed.)

    4. The emergency call service operation and contract conditions can be confirmed by accessing the G-BOOK center and performing the following maintenance checks:

      Maintenance Check
      		Outline
      Automatic Maintenance Check
      		The telematics transceiver automatically and periodically accesses the emergency call center to confirm if the user is still registered.
      Manual Maintenance Check
      		The user can press the telephone switch assembly for 10 seconds or more while the green and red indicator lights are on immediately after the engine switch is turned on (ACC) to access the G-BOOK center and confirm that they are still registered.

      HINT:
      In the following cases, the emergency call service may not be available or the service quality may suffer.
      1. The G-BOOK online service has not been established or the first manual maintenance check has not been completed.
      2. Communication is interrupted due to a malfunction of the telematics transceiver or any other related device.
      3. The location cannot be determined.
      4. Communication is interrupted due to a service provider system malfunction.
      5. Communication problems originate in the communication network used for the emergency call system.
      6. The emergency call service is suspended or halted due to emergency call provider reasons.
      7. Although a manual call is made, the situation cannot be confirmed because the user cannot respond to questions from the G-BOOK center.



  2. Theft Tracking Service

    1. The theft tracking service notifies the G-BOOK center that the alarm has operated when the theft deterrent system is activated.

    2. The G-BOOK center sends an e-mail message and makes a call to the user to notify them that the alarm has operated.



    3. In the event of a theft, the vehicle location can be tracked as requested by the user. If requested by the user, a G-BOOK operator will ask the authorities to find the stolen vehicle and take the case under their control.

  3. Remote Maintenance Service

    1. Remote maintenance mail and warning notifications are provided as part of the remote maintenance service.

    2. The remote maintenance mail is sent by the telematics transceiver to the G-BOOK center to provide information about the condition of the vehicle. The G-BOOK center then notifies the vehicle user about the inspection timing and maintenance notice.



    3. The warning notification is a signal indicating a failure or malfunction of systems, information about which is received by the combination meter assembly. This notification is sent by the telematics transceiver to the G-BOOK center. The G-BOOK center then notifies the vehicle user about the malfunction condition and how to cope with the problem based on the information received.




      No.

      2

      3
      		The user makes a reservation by making a call.
      4

      5

      6

      7

  4. Operator Service

    1. The operator service provides the user with a direct call to a G-BOOK center operator. The user can ask the operator to set a destination or send a POI facility search result to the navigation system.

    2. The operator service uses the telematics transceiver to make a call (it is not necessary to have registered a phone using the Bluetooth hands-free function).

      Contents
      		Outline
      Destination Setting
      		Displays the specified destination location on the navigation system screen.
      Nearby POI Facility Guidance
      		Displays the detailed information and location of the specified facilities on the navigation system screen.

  5. Browser Content Display Function (Information Service)

    1. Using the browser content display function built in the navigation system, the following contents can be displayed. If news content is displayed, headlines are read out to assist the driver in checking the content easily even while driving.

      Contents
      		Outline
      Weather Forecast
      		Displays and reads out the vehicle location, destination or weather forecast for the specified area.
      News
      		Displays and reads out updates, society, sports and entertainment news.

  6. Navigation System Link Function

    1. The navigation system link function provides the navigation system with real-time information obtained from the G-BOOK center, offering more effective use of the navigation system for the user.

    2. The navigation system link function has the following functions:

      Function
      		Outline
      G Route Search
      		Searches for the most suitable route to the destination based on information about wide-area traffic congestion prediction.
      Facility Information Display
      		Displays information about facilities which are not included in the navigation system.
      G Information Mark Display
      		Displays site information from the G-BOOK.com website.
      G Information Mark Linked Service
      		After obtaining information from the G-BOOK center for a facility that has guidance information, the information is read aloud when the vehicle nears the location of the facility to which the adjacent guidance information applies.



  7. G Route Search

    1. The G-BOOK center calculates traffic congestion conditions based on driving conditions of vehicles equipped with G-BOOK to offer users probe communication traffic information. Such real-time traffic information can be obtained by using the G route search.



    2. The G route search function uses information about wide-area traffic congestion prediction (information about traffic around the present location, expressways and general roads) based on probe communication traffic information in order to search for the most suitable route to the destination.




      No.
      		Outline
      (1)
      		A vehicle equipped with G-BOOK is driven on a road for which Real-time Traffic Information of China (RTIC)* is not provided. The vehicle sends driving information (such as traffic congestion level and time required) to the G-BOOK center.
      (2)
      		A vehicle equipped with G-BOOK uses the G route search function.
      (3)
      		Based on the traffic information sent from vehicles equipped with G-BOOK, the G-BOOK center sends traffic congestion information and information about a route that can be taken to avoid the traffic congestion.
      (4)
      		In addition to displaying traffic congestion information on the user's navigation map screen (as specified in (2) above), it is also possible to search for a route while considering the traffic congestion. (It is possible to enhance the accuracy of the expected time of arrival and search for the most direct route to the destination.)

      HINT:
      *: Traffic information from the Real-time Traffic information of China.
