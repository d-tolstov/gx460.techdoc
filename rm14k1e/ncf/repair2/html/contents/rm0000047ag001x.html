PARKING ASSIST MONITOR SYSTEM > DETAILS



FUNCTION OF MAIN COMPONENTS

Component
Function
Parking Assist ECU
Transmits visual signals, which are composed of a view of the area behind the vehicle taken with the television camera and parking assist guide lines, to the display and navigation module display. Transmits visual signals, which are composed of a view of the front passenger side area of the vehicle taken with the side television camera and path lines, to the display and navigation module display.
Rear Television Camera Assembly
Captures images in rear of the vehicle and outputs visual signals to parking assist ECU.
Skid Control ECU
Transmits received vehicle speed signals to the parking assist ECU.
Steering Angle Sensor
Detects the steering angle and transmits signals to the parking assist ECU.
Display and Navigation Module Display
Receives image signals from the parking assist ECU and displays these images on the display.
Park/Neutral Position Switch Assembly
Transmits a signal telling the ECM that the shift lever is in R.
ECM
Sends received shift position signals to the parking assist ECU.

OPERATING CONDITION

  1. Parking assist monitor system operates when both of the following conditions are met:

    1. The engine switch is on (IG) or on (ACC).
    2. The shift lever is in R.


FUNCTION

  1. Area Displayed on Screen

    1. Television Camera

      1. On the display, objects on the right of the vehicle appear on the right side of the display panel, and objects on the left of the vehicle appear on the left side of the display panel.

      2. The television camera uses a wide-angle lens. The perceived distance from images that appear on the screen differs from the actual distance.



        HINT:
        The area displayed on the screen may vary in accordance with vehicle status or road conditions. The area covered by the television camera is limited. The television camera does not show objects close to either corner of the bumper or under the bumper.

  2. Manual Assist

    1. Three different manual assist modes can be selected from: parking guide line mode, guide deletion mode, and estimated guide line mode. The initial (default) setting for manual assist is the parking guide line mode.

      1. The mode can be changed by touching the button located at the lower-left corner of the screen.

      2. When the manual assist mode is operating, fixed guide lines appear superimposed on a view of the area behind the vehicle. These guide lines can be used to assist the driver while backing up the vehicle.




        Item

  3. Parallel Parking Assist



    1. The parallel parking assist can select the following two display modes: standard mode and narrow mode.

      1. The mode can be changed by touching the button located at the lower-left corner of the screen.
      2. The narrow mode is designed for situations in which the parking space is narrower than the standard mode.

      3. The parallel parking assist is set to standard mode by default.

  4. Warning Message

    1. A warning message appears at the bottom or the center of the screen under the conditions listed below, regardless of whether the system is in the manual or parallel parking assist mode. The warning message appears in the language that has been selected by the language selector of the multi display.

      Warning Message Appearing at Bottom or Side of Screen 
      Warning Message
      		Outline
      Check surroundings for safety
      		This message always appears during system operation.
      System Initializing
      		This message appears when the system is restarted if the parking assist ECU detects that a battery terminal has been disconnected and subsequently reconnected.
      Guidance Unavailable
      1. This message appears when the vehicle backs up even after the message of "Steering wheel turned too far, please return." appears in the display. In this case, the system judges that the assist operation is impossible.
      2. This message is displayed when an error in the steering sensor or a gyro signal error of the navigation ECU occurs.

      Warning Message Appearing at Center Screen 
      Warning Message
      		Outline
      System not ready
      		This message appears if the system is started without initializing after a part is replaced.
      Steering wheel turned too far, please return
      		This message is displayed when the system cannot perform the assist operation under the following situations:
      1. When the vehicle starts in the parallel parking assist mode, the distance between the vehicle and the other parked vehicles is more than approximately 1 m (3.2 ft.) too far.
      2. The steering wheel is turned too far.

  5. Adjust Function during Part Replacement

    1. The items listed below must always be adjusted whenever one of the following conditions occurs. For details, refer to the corresponding Repair Manual for this model.

      1. Television Camera Optical Axis Adjustment
      2. Parking Assist ECU Initializing

  2. Estimated Guide Line Mode

    1. To use the estimated guide line mode for manual assist to park the vehicle in the parking space illustrated below, perform the following procedure:

      1. Move the shift lever to R.
      2. Select manual assist mode.
      3. The estimated guide line mode is selected.

      4. An image appears on the display panel as illustrated below:




      5. Turn the steering wheel so that the estimated guide lines are within the parking space, and back up carefully.



      6. When the rear of the vehicle is within the parking space, turn the steering wheel in order to equalize the left and right intervals between the vehicle width extension guide lines and the parking space sidelines.

      7. When the vehicle width extension guide lines and the parking space sidelines are parallel, straighten the steering wheel and then carefully back up until the entire vehicle is within the parking space.



  3. Parallel Parking Assist Mode

    1. To use the parallel parking assist mode to park the vehicle in the parking space illustrated below, perform the following procedure:

      1. Stop the vehicle parallel to the road or the curb, at a side-to-side clearance of approximately 1 m (3.3 ft.) from the parked vehicle, approximately one-half car length forward of the parked vehicle.
      2. Aim the steering wheel straight ahead and move the shift lever to R.
      3. The parallel parking assist mode is selected.

DIAGNOSIS

  1. The parking assist monitor system is equipped with a diagnosis function which can display the service menus. The method for entering the service menu screen is the same as the method used for the display and navigation module display. For details, refer to the corresponding Repair Manual for this model.
