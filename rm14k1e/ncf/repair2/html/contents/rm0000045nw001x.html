SYSTEM CONTROL

  1. The wiper and washer system has the following functions:

    Function
    		Outline
    Wiper
    		Vehicle Speed-sensing, Adjustable Interval
    		This function controls the wiper interval time in accordance with the vehicle speed when the windshield wiper switch assembly is in the AUTO position.
    Vehicle Speed Switching
    		Automatically selects an intermittent operation when the vehicle is stopped with the windshield wiper switch assembly in the low position.
    Stopped Vehicle Intermittent Wiper
    		Automatically selects an intermittent operation when the windshield wiper switch assembly is switched to the low position from any position other than low with the vehicle stopped.
    Raindrop Sensing
    		Controls the wiping interval and speed in accordance with the amount of rain, the vehicle speed and taillight signals when the windshield wiper switch assembly is in the AUTO position.
    Washer-linked Wiper with Drip-prevention
    		Operates the wipers once after they have operated in unison with the washer, to prevent the fluid from dripping after the washer has been operated.
    Washer
    		Headlight Cleaner
    		Sprays washer fluid on the headlight lenses to clean them.
    Washer Fluid Level Warning
    		When the volume of washer fluid decreases to below a certain level, the combination meter warns the driver through the master warning light illumination and the warning message on the multi-information display.



  2. Vehicle Speed-sensing, Adjustable Interval Function

    1. When the windshield wiper switch assembly is in the AUTO position, the interval time of the wiper is controlled in 3 stages in accordance with the automatic control adjuster position and the vehicle speed.



    2. The interval time can be controlled steplessly within each range.





  3. Vehicle Speed Switching Function

    1. When the windshield wiper switch assembly is in the low position and the vehicle has stopped running, wiper operation is automatically switched to intermittent operation.

    2. The front wiper relay circuit judges that the vehicle is being driven if one of the following conditions is met:

      1. The vehicle speed is anything other than 0 km/h (0 mph).
      2. The stop light switch and the parking brake switch are off and the shift lever is in a position other than P or N.

    3. If other conditions exist, the front wiper relay circuit judges that the vehicle is stopped. It controls the wipers as shown in the timing chart below.



    4. When the vehicle stops running and the windshield wiper switch assembly is in the low position, the wipers operate twice in low. They then switch to intermittent operation with an interval time of approximately 2.5 seconds. If the vehicle starts driving during this intermittent wiper operation, the wiper returns automatically to low operation.





  4. Stopped Vehicle Intermittent Wiper Function

    1. When the windshield wiper switch assembly is changed to the low position while the vehicle is stopped, the wipers operate 3 times in low. They then switch automatically to an intermittent operation with an interval time of approximately 2.5 seconds.



    2. When the automatic control adjuster is in the fast position, the wipers operate in low when the vehicle is stopped.



  5. Raindrop Sensing Function

    1. The raindrop sensing function controls the wiper timing in accordance with the amount of rain that strikes the windshield when the windshield wiper switch assembly is in the AUTO position.

    2. The front wiper relay circuit controls the timing based on the signal from the rain sensor.



  6. Washer-linked Wiper with Drip-prevention Function

    1. When the windshield wiper switch assembly is set to off or AUTO and the washer switch is turned on for approximately 0.2 seconds or more, the wipers start operating in low at the same time as the washer fluid is sprayed.

    2. The wipers operate 3 times in low after the washer switch is turned off.



    3. As shown in the following diagram, after the end of this operation, the wipers operate once more. They do so after an interval determined by vehicle speed, so that any washer fluid drips are wiped away.

CONSTRUCTION


  1. Rain Sensor



    1. The rain sensor consists of a Light Emitting Diode (LED) that emits infrared rays, a photo diode that can receive those rays, a lens and rain sensor tape.



      HINT:
      If the rain sensor tape has been peeled off during a windshield glass replacement, make sure to affix new rain sensor tape. Failure to do so will lead to a system malfunction.

    2. If no raindrops are present in the detection area, the infrared rays emitted by the LED are all reflected from the outer surface of the windshield glass and are received by the photo diode.



    3. If raindrops are present in the detection area, a portion of the emitted infrared rays will penetrate the windshield glass due to the change in the difference of the index of refraction between the glass and the area outside the windshield (difference between air and water). The ability of the windshield glass to reflect all light back inside changes due to the presence of raindrops. This change of internal reflection due to the presence of water reduces the amount of infrared rays that are received by the photo diode. The amount of this reduction is then used to determine the amount of raindrops. This function then selects intermittent, low, or high wiper operation in order to operate the wipers at an optimal speed and timing.





  2. Washer Nozzle



    1. Spray type washer nozzles are located under the engine hood to ensure good appearance. These nozzles can spray windshield washer fluid over a wide area by spraying it in a fan shape. The washer fluid volume has been reduced so as not to hinder the driver's view when the washer system is operated.



      HINT:
      Spray type washer nozzles cannot be adjusted because of their structure. Do not attempt to adjust the nozzles as it could damage them. If adjustment is necessary, adjust the nozzles by replacing them with nozzles that have different spray angles. For details, refer to the corresponding Repair Manual for this model.


FAIL-SAFE

  1. The following fail-safe measures are taken if a malfunction is detected by the windshield wiper switch assembly.

    Trouble Area
    		Condition
    Wiper Motor Malfunction
    		Stops the operation of the raindrop sensing function.
    Communication Signal Malfunction
    		Rain Sensor
    		Stops the operation of the raindrop sensing function.
    When a signal cannot be received from the rain sensor, the raindrop sensing function is not performed and the status that existed prior to the occurrence of abnormality is maintained.
    Combination Meter Assembly
    1. Stops the operation of the vehicle speed-sensing, adjustable interval vehicle function.
    2. Stops the operation of the speed switching function.
    3. Stops the operation of the stopped vehicle intermittent wiper function.
    4. The wiper interval can be adjusted.
