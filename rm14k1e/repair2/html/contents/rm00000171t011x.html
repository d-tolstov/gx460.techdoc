HYDRAULIC BRAKE BOOSTER > REMOVAL

for Preparation Click here


HINT:
When pressure in the power supply system is released, the reaction force decreases and the stroke becomes longer.

NOTICE:
  1. Be sure not to allow the pump to contact the floor; support it with the booster body. If the pump contacts the floor, the red brake tube between the pump and booster body may be deformed, the operating sound of the pump may become loud enough to hear within the cabin, and fluid may leak.
  2. When installing, coat the parts indicated by arrows with lithium soap base glycol grease (Click here).
  3. As high pressure is applied to the brake actuator tube No. 1, never deform it.
  4. Do not turn the engine switch on (IG) until the procedures are completed.
  5. Before starting the work, make sure that the engine switch is off and depress the brake pedal more than 20 times.



1. DISCONNECT CABLE FROM NEGATIVE BATTERY TERMINAL

CAUTION:
Wait at least 90 seconds after disconnecting the cable from the negative (-) battery terminal to disable the SRS system.

NOTICE:
  1. w/ Navigation System (for HDD):
    After the engine switch is turned off, the display and navigation module display (HDD navigation system) records various types of memory and settings. As a result, after turning the engine switch off, be sure to wait for the time specified in the following table before disconnecting the cable from the negative (-) battery terminal.

    Waiting Time before Disconnecting Cable from Negative (-) Battery Terminal:

    Condition
    		Waiting Time
    Vehicle enrolled in G-BOOK system
    		6 minutes
    Vehicle not enrolled in G-BOOK system
    		1 minute

  2. When disconnecting the cable, some systems need to be initialized after the cable is reconnected (Click here).



2. REMOVE ENGINE ROOM SIDE COVER LH

  1. Remove the 4 clips and engine room side cover LH.



3. DRAIN BRAKE FLUID

NOTICE:
Wash off brake fluid immediately if it comes into contact with a painted surface.



4. REMOVE LOWER NO. 1 INSTRUMENT PANEL AIRBAG ASSEMBLY

  1. Remove the lower No. 1 instrument panel airbag assembly (Click here).



5. REMOVE PUSH ROD PIN

  1. Remove the clip and push rod pin from the brake pedal.



6. REMOVE FUEL FILTER ASSEMBLY

  1. Remove the fuel filter assembly (Click here).



7. REMOVE CANISTER



  1. Disconnect the purge line hose from the canister.

  1. Disconnect the vent line hose from the canister.

  1. Disconnect the air inlet line hose from the canister.




  1. Remove the canister from the bracket.



8. REMOVE AIR INJECTION CONTROL DRIVER WITH BRACKET



  1. Disconnect the 2 air injection control driver connectors.




  1. Remove the bolt and air injection control driver with bracket.



9. REMOVE HYDRAULIC BRAKE BOOSTER ASSEMBLY



  1. Disconnect the 3 connectors from the hydraulic brake booster assembly.




  1. Detach the clamp.

  1. Remove the bolt and wiring harness bracket.




  1. Using a screwdriver, detach the VSC connector from the clamp bracket.




  1. Use tags or make a memo to identify the place to reconnect.

    HINT:
    1. *1: To front wheel cylinder RH
    2. *2: To front wheel cylinder LH
    3. *3: To rear wheel cylinder RH
    4. *4: To rear wheel cylinder LH




  1. Using a union nut wrench, disconnect the 4 brake lines from the hydraulic brake booster assembly.




  1. Disconnect the 3 brake tubes from the clamp.

  1. Disconnect the brake tube from the clamp.

    NOTICE:
    1. If excessive force is used when disconnecting the brake tube from the clamp, the clamp may brake.
    2. If the clamp breaks, do not reuse it. Replace the clamp with a new one.

  1. Remove the bolt and disconnect the clamp from the body.




  1. Remove the 4 nuts and pull out the hydraulic brake booster assembly.



10. REMOVE BRAKE BOOSTER GASKET

  1. Remove the brake booster gasket from the hydraulic brake booster assembly.
