AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION ASSEMBLY > REMOVAL

for Preparation Click here



1. DISCONNECT CABLE FROM NEGATIVE BATTERY TERMINAL

NOTICE:
  1. w/ Navigation System (for HDD):
    After the engine switch is turned off, the display and navigation module display (HDD navigation system) records various types of memory and settings. As a result, after turning the engine switch off, be sure to wait for the time specified in the following table before disconnecting the cable from the negative (-) battery terminal.

    Waiting Time before Disconnecting Cable from Negative (-) Battery Terminal:

    Condition
    		Waiting Time
    Vehicle enrolled in G-BOOK system
    		6 minutes
    Vehicle not enrolled in G-BOOK system
    		1 minute

  2. When disconnecting the cable, some systems need to be initialized after the cable is reconnected (Click here).



2. REMOVE NO. 1 ENGINE UNDER COVER SUB-ASSEMBLY

  1. Remove the 4 bolts.




  1. Unhook the No. 1 engine under cover from the vehicle body as shown in the illustration.



3. REMOVE REAR ENGINE UNDER COVER ASSEMBLY

  1. Remove the 4 bolts and rear engine under cover.



4. REMOVE PROPELLER SHAFT HEAT INSULATOR



  1. Remove the 2 bolts and propeller shaft heat insulator.



5. DRAIN AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION FLUID

  1. Remove the drain plug and gasket, and drain the ATF.

  1. Install a new gasket and the drain plug.

    Torque:
    20 N*m{ 204 kgf*cm , 15 ft.*lbf }



6. DRAIN ENGINE COOLANT

CAUTION:
Do not remove the radiator cap while the engine and radiator are still hot. Pressurized, hot engine coolant and steam may be released and cause serious burns.

HINT:
Collect the coolant in a container and dispose of it according to the regulations in your area.

  1. Loosen the radiator drain cock plug.

  1. Remove the radiator cap. Then drain the coolant from the radiator.

  1. Loosen the 2 cylinder block drain cock plugs. Then drain the coolant from the engine.



  1. Tighten the 2 cylinder block drain cock plugs.




    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Radiator Reservoir
    		*2
    		Radiator Cap
    *3
    		Cylinder Block Drain Cock Plug
    		*4
    		Radiator Drain Cock Plug

    Torque:
    13 N*m{ 130 kgf*cm , 9 ft.*lbf }

  1. Tighten the radiator drain cock plug by hand.



7. REMOVE FRONT PROPELLER SHAFT ASSEMBLY

  1. Remove the front propeller shaft (Click here).



8. REMOVE PROPELLER SHAFT ASSEMBLY

  1. Remove the propeller shaft (Click here).



9. REMOVE FRONT EXHAUST PIPE ASSEMBLY

  1. Remove the front exhaust pipe (Click here).



10. DISCONNECT TRANSMISSION CONTROL CABLE ASSEMBLY



  1. Remove the nut and clip and disconnect the transmission control cable from the transmission.



11. REMOVE STARTER ASSEMBLY

  1. Remove the starter (Click here).



12. REMOVE DRIVE PLATE AND TORQUE CONVERTER CLUTCH SETTING BOLT



  1. Remove the flywheel housing side cover.




  1. Turn the crankshaft to gain access to the 6 bolts and remove each bolt while holding the crankshaft pulley bolt with a wrench.



13. SUPPORT AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION ASSEMBLY



  1. Support the transmission with a transmission jack. Lift the transmission slightly from the crossmember.



14. REMOVE FRONT SUSPENSION MEMBER BRACKET LH AND RH



  1. Remove the 8 bolts and front suspension member bracket LH and RH.



15. REMOVE NO. 3 FRAME CROSSMEMBER SUB-ASSEMBLY



  1. Remove the 4 bolts of the rear engine mounting insulator.




  1. Remove the 4 nuts, 4 bolts and frame crossmember.



16. REMOVE REAR NO. 1 ENGINE MOUNTING INSULATOR



  1. Remove the bolt and rear engine mounting heat insulator.

  1. Remove the 4 bolts and rear engine mounting insulator from the transmission.



17. DISCONNECT WIRE HARNESS AND CONNECTOR



  1. Remove the nut and disconnect the ground cable.




  1. Tilt the transmission downward.

    NOTICE:
    Make sure the cooling fan does not contact the fan shroud.



  1. Remove the 2 nuts and No. 2 wire harness heat insulator.




    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		No. 2 Wire Harness Heat Insulator
    		-
    		-

  1. Remove the nut and disconnect the crankshaft position sensor connector and sensor harness.

  1. Disconnect the park/neutral position switch connector, transmission wire connector, 2 speed sensor connectors and transfer control connector.

    HINT:
    Detach the claw, press down the lever, and then disconnect the transmission wire connector.

  1. Detach the 2 connector clamps and 8 harness clamps and disconnect the wire harness.



18. DISCONNECT NO. 1 WATER BY-PASS PIPE



  1. Disconnect the 2 water by-pass hoses from the transmission oil cooler.

    NOTICE:
    Use a container to catch any coolant which flows out of the water by-pass hoses.

  1. Remove the 2 bolts and disconnect the water by-pass pipe.



19. REMOVE TRANSMISSION OIL COOLER ASSEMBLY



  1. Disconnect the No. 2 oil cooler inlet hose and No. 2 oil cooler outlet hose from the transmission oil thermostat.




  1. Disconnect the No. 1 oil cooler inlet hose and No. 1 oil cooler outlet hose from each oil cooler tube union.

  1. Remove the 3 bolts and transmission oil cooler with transmission oil thermostat.



20. REMOVE AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION ASSEMBLY



  1. Remove the 10 bolts and transmission.

    NOTICE:
    Do not use excess force when prying the transmission assembly.



21. REMOVE HARNESS CLAMP BRACKET

  1. Remove the 7 bolts and 7 brackets.



22. REMOVE NO. 1 TRANSMISSION CONTROL CABLE BRACKET



  1. Remove the 2 bolts and bracket.
