INSPECTION PROCEDURE

1.IDENTIFY COMPONENT SHOWN BY SUB-CODE


  1. Enter the diagnostic mode.



  1. Press the preset switch "channel 3" to change to "Detailed Information Mode".

  1. Identify the component shown by the sub-code.

    HINT:
    1. "190 (radio receiver assembly)" is the component shown by the sub-code in the example shown in the illustration.
    1. For details of the DTC display, refer to DTC Check/Clear (Click here).


NEXT


2.CHECK COMPONENT SHOWN BY SUB-CODE

  1. Select the component which has stored this code.

    HINT:
    The "Bluetooth" handsfree module is built into the radio receiver assembly. If there is a problem between the "Bluetooth" handsfree module and radio receiver assembly, replace the radio receiver assembly.

    Component Table 
    Component
    		Proceed to
    Except radio receiver assembly
    		A
    Radio receiver assembly (190)
    		B



B
REPLACE RADIO RECEIVER ASSEMBLY (Click here)
A


3.CHECK POWER SOURCE CIRCUIT OF COMPONENT SHOWN BY SUB-CODE

  1. Inspect the power source circuit of the component shown by the sub-code.

    HINT:
    If the power source circuit is operating normally, proceed to the next step.


    Component Table 
    Component
    		Proceed to
    Stereo component amplifier assembly (440)
    		Stereo Component Amplifier Power Source Circuit (Click here)
    Multi-media interface ECU (388)
    		Multi-media Interface ECU Power Source Circuit (Click here)


NEXT


4.INSPECT RADIO RECEIVER ASSEMBLY



  1. Disconnect the H29 and H28 radio receiver assembly connectors.

  1. Measure the resistance according to the value(s) in the table below.

    Standard Resistance:
    Tester Connection
    		Condition
    		Specified Condition
    H29-5 (ATX+) - H29-15 (ATX-)
    		Always
    		60 to 80 Ω
    H28-9 (TXM+) - H28-10 (TXM-)
    		Always
    		60 to 80 Ω



NG
REPLACE RADIO RECEIVER ASSEMBLY (Click here)
OK


5.CHECK HARNESS AND CONNECTOR (RADIO RECEIVER - COMPONENT SHOWN BY SUB-CODE)

HINT:
  1. Start the check from the circuit that is near the component shown by the sub-code first.
  1. For details of the connectors, refer to Terminals of ECU (Click here).

  1. Referring to the wiring diagram below, check the AVC-LAN circuit between the radio receiver assembly and the component shown by the sub-code.

    1. Disconnect all connectors between the radio receiver assembly and the component shown by the sub-code.

    2. Check for an open or short in the AVC-LAN circuit between the radio receiver assembly and the component shown by the sub-code.

      OK:
      There is no open or short circuit.



NG
REPAIR OR REPLACE HARNESS OR CONNECTOR
OK


6.REPLACE COMPONENT SHOWN BY SUB-CODE

  1. Replace the component shown by the sub-code with a normal one and check if the same problem occurs again.

    OK:
    The same problem does not occur.

REPLACE RADIO RECEIVER ASSEMBLY (Click here)
