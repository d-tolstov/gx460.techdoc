REAR STABILIZER BAR > REMOVAL

for Preparation Click here



1. REMOVE SIDE STEP ASSEMBLY LH



  1. Disconnect the connector and detach the clamp.
    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Clamp

  1. Remove the 6 bolts.




  1. Put protective tape around the side step.
    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Protective Tape

  1. Using a T30 "TORX" socket wrench, remove the 2 screws.

  1. Remove the 3 screws.

  1. Detach the 6 clips and remove the side step.



2. REMOVE STABILIZER CONTROL VALVE PROTECTOR



  1. Remove the 2 bolts and stabilizer control valve protector.



3. DRAIN SUSPENSION FLUID



  1. Loosen the bleeder plug on the stabilizer control with accumulator housing assembly and drain suspension fluid.

    HINT:
    1. Drain suspension fluid when performing operations related to the hydraulic circuits.
    2. Draining suspension fluid decreases suspension fluid pressure.

  1. Tighten the bleeder plug.

    Torque:
    8.3 N*m{ 85 kgf*cm , 73 in.*lbf }



4. REMOVE REAR STABILIZER LOWER BRACKET



  1. Remove the 4 bolts and 2 rear stabilizer lower brackets.



5. REMOVE REAR STABILIZER BAR SUB-ASSEMBLY



  1. Remove the 2 bolts, 2 nuts and rear stabilizer bar.

    NOTICE:
    Turn the bolts while holding the nuts.

  1. Remove the 2 stabilizer bushes from the rear stabilizer bar.



6. DISCONNECT REAR STABILIZER CONTROL TUBE ASSEMBLY



  1. Remove the 2 union bolts and 2 pressure port gaskets and disconnect the rear stabilizer control tube from the rear stabilizer control cylinder.



7. REMOVE REAR STABILIZER CONTROL CYLINDER



  1. Remove the bolt, nut and rear stabilizer control cylinder.

    NOTICE:
    Do not hold the rear stabilizer control cylinder by the cylinder boot.

    HINT:
    Turn the nut while holding the bolt.

  1. Remove the 2 bleeder plug caps from the rear stabilizer control cylinder.



8. REMOVE REAR STABILIZER LINK ASSEMBLY



  1. Remove the bolt, nut and rear stabilizer link assembly.

    HINT:
    Turn the nut while holding the bolt.
