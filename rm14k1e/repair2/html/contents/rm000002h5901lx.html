1. INSTALL POWER STEERING OIL PRESSURE SWITCH

  1. Install a new O-ring to the power steering oil pressure switch.

  1. Apply a light coat of engine oil to the O-ring.




  1. Using SST, install the power steering oil pressure switch.

    SST
    09922-10010  

    Torque:
    without SST:
    21 N*m{ 210 kgf*cm , 15 ft.*lbf }
    with SST:
    15 N*m{ 148 kgf*cm , 11 ft.*lbf }

    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Fulcrum Length

    HINT:
    1. Use a torque wrench with a fulcrum length of 30 cm (11.8 in.). When using a torque wrench with a fulcrum length that is not 30 cm (11.8 in.), calculate the torque specification for the torque wrench and SST based on the "without SST" torque specification (Click here).
    2. Make sure SST and the torque wrench are connected in a straight line.

  1. Connect the power steering oil pressure switch connector.



2. INSTALL FAN AND GENERATOR V BELT

  1. Install the fan and generator V belt (Click here).



3. ADD POWER STEERING FLUID


4. BLEED AIR FROM POWER STEERING SYSTEM

  1. Check the fluid level.

  1. Jack up the front of the vehicle and support it with stands.

  1. Turn the steering wheel.

    1. With the engine stopped, turn the wheel slowly from lock to lock several times.

  1. Lower the vehicle.

  1. Start the engine.

    1. Run the engine at idle for a few minutes.

  1. Turn the steering wheel.

    1. With the engine idling, turn the wheel to the left or right full lock position and keep it there for 2 to 3 seconds, then turn the wheel to the opposite full lock position and keep it there for 2 to 3 seconds.*1

    2. Repeat *1 several times.

  1. Stop the engine.




  1. Check for foaming or emulsification.
    Text in Illustration 
    *a
    		CORRECT
    *b
    		INCORRECT
    If the system has to be bled twice specifically because of foaming or emulsification, check for fluid leaks in the power steering system.

  1. Check the fluid level.



5. INSPECT FOR POWER STEERING FLUID LEAK


6. CHECK POWER STEERING FLUID LEVEL



  1. Keep the vehicle horizontal.

  1. With the engine stopped, check the fluid level in the reservoir.
    If necessary, add fluid.

    Fluid:
    ATF "DEXRON" II or III, or equivalent.

    HINT:
    If the fluid is hot (over 40°C (104°F)), make sure that the fluid level is within the HOT range on the reservoir. If the fluid is cold, make sure that it is within the COLD range.

  1. Start the engine and run it at idle.

  1. Turn the steering wheel from lock to lock several times to raise the fluid temperature.

    Fluid temperature:
    80°C (176°F)




  1. Check for foaming or emulsification.
    Text in Illustration 
    *a
    		CORRECT
    *b
    		INCORRECT
    If foaming or emulsification is identified, bleed air from the power steering system.




  1. With the engine idling, measure the fluid level in the reservoir.
    Text in Illustration 
    *a
    		Engine Idling
    *b
    		Engine Stopped
    *c
    		5 mm or less

  1. Stop the engine.

  1. Wait a few minutes and remeasure the fluid level in the reservoir.

    Maximum increase in fluid level:
    Approximately 5 mm (0.197 in.)

    If the fluid level rise is more than the maximum, bleed air from the power steering system.

  1. Check the fluid level.
