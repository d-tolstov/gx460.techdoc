STABILIZER CONTROL VALVE (w/ KDSS) > REMOVAL

for Preparation Click here



1. DISCONNECT CABLE FROM NEGATIVE BATTERY TERMINAL

CAUTION:
Wait at least 90 seconds after disconnecting the cable from the negative (-) battery terminal to disable the SRS system.

NOTICE:
  1. w/ Navigation System (for HDD):
    After the engine switch is turned off, the display and navigation module display (HDD navigation system) records various types of memory and settings. As a result, after turning the engine switch off, be sure to wait for the time specified in the following table before disconnecting the cable from the negative (-) battery terminal.

    Waiting Time before Disconnecting Cable from Negative (-) Battery Terminal:

    Condition
    		Waiting Time
    Vehicle enrolled in G-BOOK system
    		6 minutes
    Vehicle not enrolled in G-BOOK system
    		1 minute

  2. When disconnecting the cable, some systems need to be initialized after the cable is reconnected (Click here).



2. REMOVE FRONT WHEEL LH


3. REMOVE REAR WHEEL LH


4. REMOVE SIDE STEP ASSEMBLY LH



  1. Disconnect the connector and detach the clamp.
    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Clamp

  1. Remove the 6 bolts.




  1. Put protective tape around the side step.
    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Protective Tape

  1. Using a T30 "TORX" socket wrench, remove the 2 screws.

  1. Remove the 3 screws.

  1. Detach the 6 clips and remove the side step.



5. REMOVE NO. 1 ENGINE UNDER COVER SUB-ASSEMBLY

  1. Remove the 4 bolts.




  1. Unhook the No. 1 engine under cover from the vehicle body as shown in the illustration.



6. REMOVE STABILIZER CONTROL VALVE PROTECTOR



  1. Remove the 2 bolts and stabilizer control valve protector.



7. DRAIN SUSPENSION FLUID



  1. Loosen the bleeder plug on the stabilizer control with accumulator housing assembly and drain suspension fluid.

    HINT:
    1. Drain suspension fluid when performing operations related to the hydraulic circuits.
    2. Draining suspension fluid decreases suspension fluid pressure.

  1. Tighten the bleeder plug.

    Torque:
    8.3 N*m{ 85 kgf*cm , 73 in.*lbf }



8. REMOVE FRONT STABILIZER TUBE PROTECTOR



  1. Remove the 2 bolts and front stabilizer tube protector.



9. REMOVE FRONT STABILIZER CONTROL TUBE INSULATOR



  1. Remove the 3 bolts and front stabilizer control tube insulator.



10. REMOVE FRONT STABILIZER CONTROL TUBE




  1. Using a union nut wrench, disconnect the 4 flare nuts.

  1. Remove the 3 bolts and front stabilizer control tubes.



11. REMOVE REAR STABILIZER CONTROL TUBE




  1. Using a union nut wrench, disconnect the 2 flare nuts.

  1. Remove the 2 union bolts of the rear stabilizer control cylinder and the 2 pressure port gaskets.

  1. Remove the 5 bolts and rear stabilizer control tubes.



12. REMOVE STABILIZER CONTROL WITH ACCUMULATOR HOUSING ASSEMBLY



  1. Disconnect the connector, and then detach the clamp from the sensor bracket.

  1. Remove the 3 bolts and stabilizer control with accumulator housing.

    NOTICE:
    Do not disassemble the stabilizer control with accumulator housing assembly or expose it to an open flame as the accumulator of the stabilizer control with accumulator housing assembly contains gas.
