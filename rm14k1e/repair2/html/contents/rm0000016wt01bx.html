FRONT DRIVE SHAFT ASSEMBLY > INSTALLATION

for Preparation Click here



1. INSTALL FRONT DRIVE SHAFT ASSEMBLY



  1. Coat the spline of the inboard joint shaft assembly with ATF.

  1. Align the shaft splines and install the drive shaft with a brass bar and hammer.

    NOTICE:
    1. Set the snap ring with the opening side facing downward.
    2. Be careful not to damage the oil seal, boot and dust cover.

    HINT:
    Whether the inboard joint shaft is in contact with the pinion shaft or not can be confirmed from the sound or feeling when driving it.



2. INSTALL FRONT SPEED SENSOR

  1. Install the speed sensor with the bolt.

    Torque:
    8.5 N*m{ 87 kgf*cm , 75 in.*lbf }

    NOTICE:
    1. Make sure there are no pieces of iron or other foreign matter attached to the sensor tip.
    2. While inserting the speed sensor into the knuckle hole, do not strike or damage the sensor tip.
    3. After installing the speed sensor, make sure there is no clearance or foreign matter between the sensor stay part and the knuckle.
    4. Make sure there is no foreign matter attached to the speed sensor rotor.



3. INSTALL FRONT LOWER BALL JOINT ATTACHMENT LH

  1. Install the front lower ball joint attachment with the 2 bolts.

    Torque:
    160 N*m{ 1632 kgf*cm , 118 ft.*lbf }



4. CONNECT TIE ROD END SUB-ASSEMBLY LH

  1. Connect the tie rod end to the steering knuckle with the nut.

    Torque:
    91 N*m{ 928 kgf*cm , 67 ft.*lbf }

    NOTICE:
    Tighten the nut up to an additional 60° if the holes for the cotter pin are not aligned.

  1. Install a new cotter pin.



5. INSTALL FRONT AXLE SHAFT NUT

  1. Clean the threaded parts on the drive shaft and axle shaft nut using a non-residue solvent.

  1. Install the front axle shaft nut.

    Torque:
    235 N*m{ 2396 kgf*cm , 173 ft.*lbf }

  1. Install the adjusting cap and a new cotter pin.



6. INSTALL FRONT AXLE HUB GREASE CAP

  1. Install the axle hub grease cap.

    NOTICE:
    Make sure the grease cap is securely installed to the axle hub.



7. FILL UP DIFFERENTIAL OIL

  1. Remove the differential filler plug and gasket.




  1. Pour oil into the differential carrier assembly so that the oil level is within 0 to 5 mm (0 to 0.197 in.) of the bottom of the filler plug opening.

    NOTICE:
    Excessively large or small quantities of oil may cause problems.


    Front Differential Capacity:

    Oil Type and Viscosity
    		Specified Condition
    Toyota Genuine Differential gear oil LT SAE 75W-85 API GL-5 or equivalent
    		1.35 to 1.45 liters (1.43 to 1.53 US qts, 1.18 to 1.27 Imp. qts.)


    Rear Differential Capacity:

    Oil Type and Viscosity
    		Specified Condition
    Toyota Genuine Differential gear oil LT SAE 75W-85 API GL-5 or equivalent
    		2.65 to 2.75 liters (2.80 to 2.91 US qts, 2.33 to 2.42 Imp. qts.)

  1. Install a new gasket and the differential filler plug.

    Torque:
    Front differential carrier:
    39 N*m{ 400 kgf*cm , 29 ft.*lbf }
    Rear differential carrier:
    49 N*m{ 500 kgf*cm , 36 ft.*lbf }

  1. Drive the vehicle and check the oil level again.
    If necessary, add differential oil.



8. INSPECT DIFFERENTIAL OIL

  1. Stop the vehicle on a level surface.

  1. Remove the differential filler plug and gasket.




  1. Check that the oil level is within 0 to 5 mm (0 to 0.197 in.) of the bottom of the filler plug opening.

    NOTICE:
    Excessively large or small quantities of oil may cause problems.

    If the oil level is low, add differential oil to adjust the oil level.
    If the oil level is still low, check for oil leakage. If leakage is found, repair or replace the part necessary to stop the leakage.

  1. Install a new gasket and differential filler plug.

    Torque:
    Front differential carrier:
    39 N*m{ 400 kgf*cm , 29 ft.*lbf }
    Rear differential carrier:
    49 N*m{ 500 kgf*cm , 36 ft.*lbf }
