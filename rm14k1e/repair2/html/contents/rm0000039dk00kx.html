1. DISCONNECT CABLE FROM NEGATIVE BATTERY TERMINAL

NOTICE:
  1. w/ Navigation System (for HDD):
    After the engine switch is turned off, the display and navigation module display (HDD navigation system) records various types of memory and settings. As a result, after turning the engine switch off, be sure to wait for the time specified in the following table before disconnecting the cable from the negative (-) battery terminal.

    Waiting Time before Disconnecting Cable from Negative (-) Battery Terminal:

    Condition
    		Waiting Time
    Vehicle enrolled in G-BOOK system
    		6 minutes
    Vehicle not enrolled in G-BOOK system
    		1 minute

  2. When disconnecting the cable, some systems need to be initialized after the cable is reconnected (Click here).

16. REMOVE NO. 3 WINDSHIELD OUTSIDE MOULDING CLIP

HINT:
Perform the following procedures if replacing any No. 3 windshield outside moulding clips.

  1. Put a 5 mm (0.157 in.) drill bit into a drill.

  1. Wind tape around the drill bit approximately 5 mm (0.197 in.) from the tip of the drill.

    HINT:
    Tape the 5 mm (0.157 in.) drill bit to prevent the drill bit from going too deep.




  1. Lightly press the drill against the No. 3 windshield outside moulding clips, drill off the flanges of the No. 3 windshield outside moulding clips, and remove them.

    NOTICE:
    1. Pressing the drill too firmly will cause the No. 3 windshield outside moulding clip to turn and result in the No. 3 windshield outside moulding clip not being drilled through.
    2. Do not pry the No. 3 windshield outside moulding clips with the drill because this may cause damage to the installation holes of the No. 3 windshield outside moulding clips or the drill bit.

    CAUTION:
    Be careful of the drilled No. 3 windshield outside moulding clips as they may become hot.

  1. Using a vacuum cleaner, remove the No. 3 windshield outside moulding clip fragments and shavings from the drilled areas.

    HINT:
    Use the same procedure to remove clips on the other side.

38. REMOVE WINDSHIELD GLASS


NOTICE:
The stoppers are located on the windshield glass sub-assembly as shown in the illustration. Be careful not to damage the windshield glass when removing the stoppers.

HINT:
Depending on the vehicle specifications, a 1-piece or 2-piece type stopper is used.

  1. From the interior, insert a piano wire between the vehicle body and glass as shown in the illustration.

  1. Tie objects that can serve as handles (for example, wooden blocks) to both wire ends.

    2. To prevent the instrument panel from being scratched when removing the glass, place a plastic sheet between the piano wire and instrument panel.

39. REMOVE WINDSHIELD GLASS ADHESIVE DAM

  1. Remove the 2 No. 1 stoppers.

    NOTICE:

43. CLEAN WINDSHIELD GLASS



  1. Using a scraper, remove the damaged stoppers, dam and adhesive sticking to the glass.

  1. Clean the outer circumference of the glass with non-residue solvent.

    NOTICE:
    1. Do not touch the surface of the glass after cleaning it.
    2. Be careful not to damage the glass.
    3. Even if using new glass, clean the glass with non-residue solvent.



44. CLEAN VEHICLE BODY



  1. Clean and shape the contact surface of the vehicle body.

    1. On the contact surface of the vehicle body, use a knife to cut away excess adhesive as shown in the illustration.

      NOTICE:
      Be careful not to damage the vehicle body.

      HINT:
      Leave as much adhesive on the vehicle body as possible.

  1. Clean the contact surface of the vehicle body with cleaner.

    HINT:
    Even if all the adhesive has been removed, clean the vehicle body.
