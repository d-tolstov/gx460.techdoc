AUTOMATIC LIGHT CONTROL SYSTEM
When the headlight dimmer switch is in the AUTO position, the automatic light control system detects ambient light levels and controls the headlights, fog lights, taillights, clearance lights and license plate lights.

LIGHT AUTO TURN-OFF SYSTEM
The light auto turn-off system is used to prevent the driver from leaving the vehicle with the headlights, fog lights, taillights, clearance lights or license plate lights on.
The lights that turn off vary as shown in the table below according to the position of the headlight dimmer switch.

Condition
Outline
Headlight dimmer switch in AUTO position
When the headlights, fog lights, clearance lights, taillights, or license plate lights are on, this system automatically turns them off when the delay time set through the customize function elapses after the engine switch is turned off and a driver side door is opened and closed.
Headlight dimmer switch in head position
When the headlights, fog lights, clearance lights, taillights, or license plate lights are on, this system automatically turns off the headlights and front fog lights when the engine switch is turned off.

HINT:
The rear fog lights*, clearance lights, taillights, and license plate lights remain on.

Headlight dimmer switch in tail position
When the fog lights, clearance lights, taillights, or license plate lights are on, this system automatically turns off the front fog lights when the engine switch is turned off.

HINT:
The rear fog lights*, clearance lights, taillights, and license plate lights remain on.

  1. *: w/ Rear Fog Light

HID HEADLIGHT SYSTEM
The High Intensity Discharge (HID) headlight system uses a discharge bulb as its light source for the low beam. Discharge bulbs are superior to halogen bulbs.
  1. Discharge bulbs have the following features:
    1. The light emitted by the bulb is close in color to sunlight. The light shines ahead over a broader area and further forward, increasing the area visible to the driver.
    2. Less power is consumed.
  2. This system consists of the discharge bulbs and light control ECUs.
  3. The light control ECU transforms voltage that is input from the battery to high voltage of up to 20000 V and applies it to the discharge bulbs in order to illuminate them.

AUTOMATIC HEADLIGHT BEAM LEVEL CONTROL SYSTEM

  1. General
    When the low beam headlights are on, the automatic headlight beam level control system operates the headlight leveling motor in accordance with the movement of the vehicle. The headlight swivel ECU controls the system.

  2. Function of Main Components

    Component
    		Outline
    Headlight Swivel ECU Assembly
    		Receives various signals, calculates changes in the vehicle posture and actuates the headlight leveling motor.
    Headlight Leveling Motor
    1. Based on the signals received from the headlight swivel ECU, the motors move the reflectors in the headlights to vary the low beam angle.
    2. Uses a stepper motor to precisely regulate the angle of the reflectors.
    Suspension Control ECU
    		Transmits a height control sensor signal to the headlight swivel ECU.
    Skid Control ECU
    		Transmits a speed sensor signal (front LH and RH) to the headlight swivel ECU.
    ECM
    		Transmits an engine running status signal to the headlight swivel ECU.
    Main Body ECU
    		Transmits a headlight status signal.
    Combination Meter Assembly
    		AFS OFF Indicator Light
    		When the system malfunctions, the combination meter assembly alerts the driver by flashing the AFS OFF indicator light in accordance with the signal from the headlight swivel ECU.

  3. Initial Set Control
    When the engine is started, the headlight swivel ECU drives the headlight leveling motor and moves the headlight reflector to its lower operational limit, and then returns it to the proper position. The headlight swivel ECU thus assesses the position of the headlight for reference control.

    NOTICE:
    1. Initialize the headlight swivel ECU after the headlight swivel ECU is replaced.
    2. Initialize the headlight swivel ECU (set the zero point of the height control sensor in the headlight swivel ECU) after the vehicle height changes due to replacement of suspension components or after performing such operations as removal and reinstallation or replacement of the rear height control sensor sub-assembly RH.


AFS (Adaptive Front-lighting System)

  1. General

    1. The Adaptive Front-lighting System (AFS) is used in order to ensure a wide low beam lighting area and realize excellent visibility during turns by moving the low beam.
    2. The AFS uses medium-to-high speed control and low speed control. In medium-to-high speed control, the system calculates the target lighting angle based on the steering angle and vehicle speed and changes the swivel angle of the low beam headlights individually. During low speed control, the system calculates the target lighting angle based on the steering angle and changes the swivel angle of the low beam headlight on the side facing into the turn.

  2. Function of Main Components

    Component
    		Outline
    Headlight Swivel ECU Assembly
    		Receives various signals, calculates the target lighting angle and actuates the headlight swivel motor.
    Headlight Swivel Motor
    1. Driven by the headlight swivel ECU, the motor moves the low beam left or right to the angle calculated by the headlight swivel ECU.
    2. A stepper motor is used for the headlight swivel motor. The headlight swivel ECU determines the low beam angle based on the number of steps (position) of the stepper motor.
    Spiral with Sensor Cable Sub-assembly (Steering Angle Sensor)
    		Detects the steering angle and direction and outputs this signal to the headlight swivel ECU.
    Steering Pad Switch Assembly
    		Pressing this switch disables the operation of the AFS.
    Skid Control ECU
    		Transmits a speed sensor signal (front LH and RH) to the headlight swivel ECU.
    ECM
    1. Transmits an engine running status signal to the headlight swivel ECU.
    2. Transmits a shift position signal to the headlight swivel ECU. The headlight swivel ECU determines whether the vehicle is moving forward or backward based on this signal.
    Main Body ECU
    		Transmits a headlight status signal.
    Combination Meter Assembly
    		AFS OFF Indicator Light
    		When the system malfunctions, the combination meter assembly flashes the AFS OFF indicator light based on the signal from the headlight swivel ECU to alert the driver.

  3. Low Speed Control

    1. The headlight swivel ECU performs low speed control when all of the following conditions are met:
      1. Engine is running.
      2. Vehicle is moving forward at a speed of 10 km/h (6 mph) or more and less than 30 km/h (19 mph).
      3. Steering angle is 6° or more.
      4. Low beam headlights are on.
      5. Daytime running light function is not operating.
      6. AFS is on.

    Swivel Angle Range 
    Driving Condition
    		Low Beam
    Left
    		Right
    Right Turn
    		0° Fixed
    		0° to 10° to Right
    Left Turn
    		0° to 15° to Left
    		0° Fixed

  4. Medium-to-High Speed Control

    1. The headlight swivel ECU performs medium-to-high speed control when all of the following conditions are met:
      1. Engine is running.
      2. Vehicle is moving forward at a speed of 30 km/h (19 mph) or more.
      3. Steering angle is 7.5° or more.
      4. Low beam headlights are on.
      5. Daytime running light function is not operating.
      6. AFS is on.

    Swivel Angle Range 
    Driving Condition
    		Low Beam
    Left
    		Right
    Right Turn
    		0° Fixed
    		0° to 10° to Right
    Left Turn
    		0° to 15° to Left
    		0° Fixed

  5. Operation Stop Condition

    1. The headlight swivel ECU stops control when any of the following conditions is met:
      1. Engine is stopped.
      2. Vehicle is moving forward at a speed of less than 10 km/h (6 mph).
      3. Steering angle is less than 6°.
      4. Low beam headlights are off.
      5. Daytime running light function is operating.
      6. AFS is off.
      7. Shift lever in R or N.

  6. Initial Set Control
    When the engine is started, the headlight swivel ECU drives the headlight swivel motor and moves the headlight reflector toward the center of the vehicle until it reaches its operational limit, and then returns it to the proper position. The headlight swivel ECU thus assesses the position of the headlight for reference control.

    NOTICE:
    Initialize the headlight swivel ECU after the headlight swivel ECU is replaced.
