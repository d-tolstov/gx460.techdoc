BRAKE PEDAL > INSTALLATION

for Preparation Click here



1. INSTALL BRAKE PEDAL SUPPORT ASSEMBLY

  1. Temporarily install the brake pedal support reinforcement set bolt.

  1. Install the hydraulic brake booster (Click here).

  1. Install the brake pedal support assembly with the 4 nuts.

    Torque:
    14 N*m{ 145 kgf*cm , 10 ft.*lbf }

  1. Tighten the brake pedal support reinforcement set bolt.

    Torque:
    20 N*m{ 204 kgf*cm , 15 ft.*lbf }



2. INSTALL PUSH ROD PIN



  1. Apply a light coat of lithium soap base glycol grease to the inner surface of the hole in the brake pedal.
    Text in Illustration 

    		Lithium soap base glycol grease




  1. Set the master cylinder push rod clevis in place, insert the push rod pin from the left side of the vehicle, and then install a new clip.



3. INSTALL BRAKE PEDAL RETURN SPRING

  1. Apply a light coat of lithium soap base glycol grease to inner surface of the hole in the brake pedal support assembly.

  1. Install the brake pedal return spring to the brake pedal support assembly.



4. INSTALL STOP LIGHT SWITCH ASSEMBLY

  1. Install the stop light switch (Click here).

  1. Connect the stop light switch connector.



5. INSTALL LOWER NO. 1 INSTRUMENT PANEL AIRBAG ASSEMBLY

  1. Install the lower No. 1 instrument panel airbag assembly (Click here).



6. CONNECT CABLE TO NEGATIVE BATTERY TERMINAL

NOTICE:
When disconnecting the cable, some systems need to be initialized after the cable is reconnected (Click here).



7. BLEED BRAKE SYSTEM

CAUTION:
If air is bled without using the intelligent tester, damage or accidents may result. Therefore, always use the intelligent tester when bleeding air.

  1. Turn the engine switch on (IG).

  1. Remove the brake master cylinder reservoir filler cap assembly.

  1. Add brake fluid until the fluid level is between the MIN and MAX lines of the reservoir.

  1. Repeatedly depress the brake pedal and bleed air from the bleeder plug of the front disc brake cylinder RH.

  1. Repeat the step above until the air is completely bled, and then tighten the bleeder plug while depressing the brake pedal.

    Torque:
    11 N*m{ 110 kgf*cm , 8 ft.*lbf }

  1. Bleed the air from the bleeder plug of the front disc brake cylinder LH using the same procedure as for the RH side.

  1. With the brake pedal depressed, loosen the bleeder plug of the rear disc brake cylinder RH, continue to hold the brake pedal and allow brake fluid to be drained from the bleeder plug while the pump motor operates.

    HINT:
    1. Air is bled as the pump motor operates while the brake pedal is being depressed.
    2. Be sure to release the brake pedal to stop the motor after approximately 100 seconds of continuous operation.
    3. As brake fluid is continuously drained while the pump operates, it is not necessary to repeatedly depress the brake pedal.

  1. When there is no more air in the brake fluid, tighten the bleeder plug, and then release the brake pedal.

    Torque:
    11 N*m{ 110 kgf*cm , 8 ft.*lbf }

  1. Bleed the air from the bleeder plug of the rear disc brake cylinder LH using the same procedure as for the RH side.

  1. Turn the engine switch off and connect the intelligent tester to the DLC3.

  1. Turn the engine switch on (IG).

  1. Turn the intelligent tester on.

  1. Enter the following menus: Chassis / ABS/VSC/TRC / Utility / Air Bleeding.

    NOTICE:
    To protect the solenoid from overheating, the solenoid operation stops automatically in 4 seconds, and then the solenoid will not respond to commands for an additional 20 seconds.

  1. Repeatedly depress the brake pedal several times, and then, with the brake pedal depressed, turn FR Line on and bleed air.

    HINT:
    Air returns to the brake master cylinder reservoir together with the brake fluid and is bled from the brake system.

    NOTICE:
    1. As it is not possible to visually confirm that air is being bled, repeat this step 10 times.
    2. Do not loosen the bleeder plug.

  1. Turn FL Line on and bleed air using the same procedures as for FR Line.

  1. Turn RR Line on, loosen the bleeder plug of the rear disc brake cylinder RH and drain brake fluid.

    HINT:
    1. Do not depress the brake pedal.
    2. As brake fluid is automatically drained while the pump and solenoid operate, it is not necessary to operate the brake pedal.

  1. Repeat the step above until the air is completely bled, and then tighten the bleeder plug.

    Torque:
    11 N*m{ 110 kgf*cm , 8 ft.*lbf }

  1. Turn RL Line on and bleed air from the bleeder plug of the rear disc brake cylinder LH using the same procedure as for the RH side.

  1. Turn the intelligent tester off and turn the engine switch off.

  1. Inspect for brake fluid leaks.

  1. Check and adjust the brake fluid level (Click here).

  1. Clear the DTCs (Click here).
