REAR BRAKE > INSTALLATION

for Preparation Click here


HINT:
  1. Use the same procedure for the RH and LH sides.
  2. The procedure listed below is for the LH side.



1. INSTALL REAR DISC

  1. Align the matchmarks and install the rear disc.

    HINT:
    When replacing the disc with a new one, select the installation position where the disc has the smallest runout.



2. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE BUSH DUST BOOT



  1. Apply a light coat of lithium soap base glycol grease to the entire circumference of a new rear disc brake bush dust boot where it contacts the rear disc brake cylinder mounting, and the entire inner circumference of both ends.
    Text in Illustration 

    		lithium soap base glycol grease

    HINT:
    Apply at least 0.3 g (0.01 oz.) of lithium soap base glycol grease to the rear disc brake bush dust boot.

    NOTICE:
    Apply a sufficient amount of lithium soap base glycol grease to the entire circumference of the rear disc brake bush dust boot and rear disc brake cylinder mounting contact surfaces.

  1. Install the rear disc brake bush dust boot to the rear disc brake cylinder mounting.



3. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE CYLINDER SLIDE BUSH



  1. Apply a light coat of lithium soap base glycol grease to a new rear disc brake cylinder slide bush.
    Text in Illustration 

    		Lithium soap base glycol grease

    HINT:
    Apply at least 0.8 g (0.03 oz.) of lithium soap base glycol grease to the rear disc brake cylinder slide bush.

  1. Install the rear disc brake cylinder slide bush to the rear disc brake cylinder mounting.




  1. Apply a light coat of lithium soap base glycol grease to the cylinder slide pin, insert it into the rear disc brake cylinder slide bush in order to properly grease the parts, and then remove it.
    Text in Illustration 

    		Lithium soap base glycol grease



4. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE CYLINDER HOLE PLUG



  1. Apply a light coat of lithium soap base glycol grease to a new rear disc brake cylinder hole plug.
    Text in Illustration 

    		Lithium soap base glycol grease

  1. Install the rear disc brake cylinder hole plug to the rear disc brake cylinder mounting.



5. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE CYLINDER MOUNTING LH

  1. Install the rear disc brake cylinder mounting and 2 plate washers with the 2 bolts.

    Torque:
    105 N*m{ 1071 kgf*cm , 77 ft.*lbf }



6. INSTALL REAR NO. 1 DISC BRAKE PAD SUPPORT PLATE

  1. Install the 2 rear No. 1 disc brake pad support plates to the rear disc brake cylinder mounting.



7. INSTALL REAR NO. 2 DISC BRAKE PAD SUPPORT PLATE

  1. Install the 2 rear No. 2 disc brake pad support plates to the rear disc brake cylinder mounting.



8. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE PAD WEAR INDICATOR PLATE

  1. Install the rear disc brake pad wear indicator plate to the inner side disc brake pad.

    HINT:
    Install the rear disc brake pad wear indicator facing downward.



9. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE ANTI-SQUEAL SHIM

NOTICE:
If necessary, replace the anti-squeal shim when replacing the brake pad.

  1. Install the rear disc brake anti-squeal shims on each rear disc brake pad.

    NOTICE:
    There should be no oil or grease on the friction surfaces of the brake pads and the rear disc.



10. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE PAD

  1. Install the 2 rear disc brake pads together with the rear disc brake anti-squeal shims to the rear disc brake cylinder mounting.



11. INSTALL REAR DISC BRAKE CYLINDER ASSEMBLY LH



  1. Apply a light coat of lithium soap base glycol grease to the sliding surfaces of the rear disc brake cylinder slide pins.
    Text in Illustration 

    		Lithium soap base glycol grease

  1. Install the rear disc brake cylinder with the 2 rear disc brake cylinder slide pins.

    Torque:
    88 N*m{ 900 kgf*cm , 65 ft.*lbf }



12. CONNECT REAR FLEXIBLE HOSE LH

  1. Connect the rear flexible hose to the rear disc brake cylinder with the union bolt and a new gasket.

    Torque:
    31 N*m{ 316 kgf*cm , 23 ft.*lbf }



13. BLEED BRAKE LINE

  1. Turn the engine switch on (IG).

  1. Remove the brake master cylinder reservoir filler cap assembly.

  1. Add brake fluid until the fluid level is between the MIN and MAX lines of the reservoir.

  1. Repeatedly depress the brake pedal and bleed air from the bleeder plug of the front disc brake cylinder RH.

  1. Repeat the step above until the air is completely bled, and then tighten the bleeder plug while depressing the brake pedal.

    Torque:
    11 N*m{ 110 kgf*cm , 8 ft.*lbf }

  1. Bleed the air from the bleeder plug of the front disc brake cylinder LH using the same procedure as for the RH side.

  1. With the brake pedal depressed, loosen the bleeder plug of the rear disc brake cylinder RH, continue to hold the brake pedal and allow brake fluid to be drained from the bleeder plug while the pump motor operates.

    HINT:
    1. Air is bled as the pump motor operates while the brake pedal is being depressed.
    2. Be sure to release the brake pedal to stop the motor after approximately 100 seconds of continuous operation.
    3. As brake fluid is continuously drained while the pump operates, it is not necessary to repeatedly depress the brake pedal.

  1. When there is no more air in the brake fluid, tighten the bleeder plug, and then release the brake pedal.

    Torque:
    11 N*m{ 110 kgf*cm , 8 ft.*lbf }

  1. Bleed the air from the bleeder plug of the rear disc brake cylinder LH using the same procedure as for the RH side.

  1. Turn the engine switch off.

  1. Inspect for brake fluid leaks.

  1. Check and adjust the brake fluid level (Click here).

  1. Clear the DTCs (Click here).
