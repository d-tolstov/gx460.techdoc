1. REMOVE TRANSMISSION CONTROL SHAFT LEVER LH



  1. Remove the nut, spring washer and control shaft lever LH.



2. REMOVE PARK/NEUTRAL POSITION SWITCH ASSEMBLY



  1. Using a screwdriver, bend the tabs of the lock washer.

  1. Remove the nut, lock washer and bolt.

  1. Remove the park/neutral position switch.



3. REMOVE OIL COOLER TUBE UNION



  1. Remove the 2 oil cooler tube unions.

  1. Remove the O-ring from each oil cooler tube union.



4. REMOVE SPEED SENSOR



  1. Remove the 2 bolts and 2 speed sensors.

  1. Remove the O-ring from each sensor.



5. REMOVE AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION BREATHER TUBE



  1. Remove the 2 bolts.

  1. Remove the breather tube.

  1. Remove the O-ring from the tube.



6. REMOVE AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION HOUSING



  1. Remove the 10 bolts and transmission housing.



7. REMOVE REAR TRANSFER ADAPTER



  1. Remove the 8 bolts and transfer adapter.

    HINT:
    Use a brass bar and hammer to remove the transfer adapter.

  1. Using a screwdriver, remove the transfer adapter hole snap ring.



8. REMOVE TRANSFER CASE ADAPTER REAR OIL SEAL



  1. Using a screwdriver, pry out the oil seal.

    NOTICE:
    Be careful not to damage the transfer adapter.

    HINT:
    Wrap the tip of the screwdriver with tape.



9. REMOVE TRANSFER CASE REAR ADAPTER OIL RECEIVER



  1. Using snap ring pliers, remove the snap ring and oil receiver.



10. FIX AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION CASE SUB-ASSEMBLY



  1. Install the transmission case to an overhaul attachment.



11. REMOVE AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION OIL PAN SUB-ASSEMBLY

  1. Remove the drain plug and gasket.

  1. Remove the overflow plug and gasket.




  1. Remove the 20 bolts and oil pan.

    NOTICE:
    Do not turn the transmission over as this will contaminate the valve body with any foreign matter in the bottom of the pan.



12. INSPECT AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION OIL PAN SUB-ASSEMBLY

  1. Remove the magnets and use them to collect steel particles.

  1. Carefully look at the foreign matter and particles in the pan and on the magnets to anticipate the type of wear you will find in the transmission.

    1. Steel (magnetic): bearing, gear and clutch plate wear
    2. Brass (non-magnetic): bush wear



13. REMOVE VALVE BODY OIL STRAINER ASSEMBLY



  1. Turn over the transmission.

  1. Remove the 4 bolts and oil strainer from the valve body.

  1. Remove the O-ring from the oil strainer.



14. REMOVE TRANSMISSION WIRE



  1. Remove the 2 bolts and 2 temperature sensor clamps.

  1. Disconnect the 2 ATF temperature sensors.

  1. Disconnect the 9 solenoid valve connectors.

  1. Detach the wire harness from the 2 clamps.




  1. Remove the bolt and pull out the transmission wire.

  1. Remove the O-ring from the transmission wire connector.



15. REMOVE TRANSMISSION VALVE BODY ASSEMBLY

  1. Remove the bolt, detent spring cover and detent spring.




  1. Remove the 19 bolts and valve body.



16. REMOVE CHECK BALL BODY



  1. Remove the check ball body and spring.

19. REMOVE C-2 ACCUMULATOR PISTON



  1. While applying compressed air to the oil hole, remove the C-2 accumulator piston and spring.

  1. Remove the 2 O-rings from the piston.

    NOTICE:
    Be careful as the C-3 and B-3 accumulator pistons may jump out.



20. REMOVE B-3 ACCUMULATOR PISTON

  1. Remove the 2 O-rings from the piston.

    NOTICE:
    Be careful as the C-3 accumulator piston may jump out.

  1. Remove the B-1 accumulator valve and 2 springs.



23. REMOVE PARKING LOCK PAWL BRACKET



  1. Remove the 3 bolts and bracket.



24. REMOVE PARKING LOCK ROD SUB-ASSEMBLY



  1. Remove the parking lock rod from the manual valve lever.



25. REMOVE PARKING LOCK PAWL SHAFT



  1. Pull out the parking lock pawl shaft from the front side to remove it, and then remove the lock pawl and spring.

  1. Remove the E-ring from the shaft.



26. REMOVE MANUAL VALVE LEVER SUB-ASSEMBLY



  1. Using a hammer and screwdriver, cut off the spacer and remove it from the shaft.




  1. Using a pin punch and hammer, tap out the spring pin.

    HINT:
    Tap out the spring pin slowly so that it does not fall into the transmission case.

  1. Pull the manual valve lever shaft out through the case and remove the manual valve lever.



27. REMOVE MANUAL VALVE LEVER SHAFT OIL SEAL



  1. Using a screwdriver, pry out the 2 oil seals.

    NOTICE:
    Be careful not to damage the transmission case.

    HINT:
    Wrap the tip of the screwdriver with tape.

  1. Remove the 10 bolts.




  1. Using SST, remove the oil pump.

    SST
    09350-30020   (09350-07020)




  1. Remove the 2 thrust bearing races and O-ring from the oil pump.



29. REMOVE CLUTCH DRUM AND INPUT SHAFT ASSEMBLY



  1. Remove the clutch drum and input shaft assembly from the transmission case.




  1. Remove the clutch drum thrust washer and 2 thrust needle roller bearings.



30. INSPECT NO. 2 1-WAY CLUTCH ASSEMBLY



  1. Hold the reverse clutch hub and turn the 1-way clutch assembly.

  1. Check that the 1-way clutch turns freely clockwise and locks when turned counterclockwise.

32. FIX CLUTCH DRUM AND INPUT SHAFT ASSEMBLY



  1. Place the oil pump onto the torque converter clutch, and then place the clutch drum and input shaft assembly onto the oil pump.



33. REMOVE REVERSE CLUTCH HUB SUB-ASSEMBLY



  1. Using a screwdriver, remove the snap ring.




  1. Remove the reverse clutch reaction sleeve and reverse clutch hub sub-assembly together with the rear clutch disc set from the clutch drum.



34. REMOVE REAR CLUTCH DISC SET

  1. Remove the clutch cushion plate, reverse clutch flange, 5 discs and 4 plates from the reverse clutch hub.




  1. Remove the reverse clutch flange from the clutch drum.



35. INSPECT REAR CLUTCH DISC



  1. Replace all discs if one of the following problems is present: 1) a disc, plate or flange is worn or burnt, 2) the lining of a disc is peeled off or discolored, or 3) the grooves or printed numbers have even a little bit of damage.

    NOTICE:
    Before assembling new discs, soak them in ATF for at least 15 minutes.



36. INSPECT REVERSE CLUTCH HUB SUB-ASSEMBLY



  1. Using a dial indicator, measure the inside diameter of the reverse clutch hub bush.

    Standard inside diameter:
    35.812 to 35.837 mm (1.410 to 1.411 in.)

    1. If the inside diameter is not as specified, replace the reverse clutch hub.



37. REMOVE FORWARD CLUTCH HUB SUB-ASSEMBLY

40. INSPECT UNDERDRIVE 1-WAY CLUTCH ASSEMBLY
