REMOVE OPTIONAL COMPONENTS AND CHECK AGAIN (SEE NOTICE ABOVE) 

3.CHECK RADIO RECEIVER ASSEMBLY



  1. Remove the antenna connector from the radio receiver assembly.

  1. Turn the engine switch on (ACC) with the radio receiver assembly connector connected.

  1. Turn the radio on and tune into AM mode.

  1. Place a screwdriver, thin wire or other metal object on the radio receiver antenna jack and check that noise can be heard from the speaker.

    OK:
    Noise occurs.

REPLACE RADIO RECEIVER ASSEMBLY (Click here)
OK


4.CHECK GLASS ANTENNA



  1. Check for continuity of the antenna.

    HINT:
    Check for continuity at the center of each antenna wire as shown in the illustration.

    NOTICE:
    When cleaning the glass, wipe it in the direction of the wire with a soft dry cloth. Take care not to damage the wire. Do not use detergents or glass cleaners with abrasive ingredients. When measuring resistance, wrap a piece of tin foil around the tip of the probe and press the foil against the wire with your finger, as shown in the illustration.

    OK:
    There is continuity in the antenna.

6.CHECK ANTENNA CORD SUB-ASSEMBLY

  1. Remove the antenna connector of the radio receiver and antenna.

  1. Measure the resistance between the antenna and radio receiver to check for an open circuit in the antenna cord.

    Standard resistance:
    Below 1 Ω

  1. Measure the resistance between the antenna cord and body ground to check for a short circuit in the antenna cord.

    Standard resistance:
    10 kΩ or higher
