REAR AXLE SHAFT > INSTALLATION

for Preparation Click here


HINT:
  1. Use the same procedure for the RH and LH sides.
  2. The procedure listed below is for the LH side.



1. INSTALL REAR AXLE SHAFT OIL SEAL LH



  1. Using SST and a hammer, install a new axle shaft oil seal.

    SST
    09950-60020   (09951-00770)
    09950-70010   (09951-07150)

    NOTICE:
    Do not allow foreign matter, etc. to contact the axle shaft housing hole.



2. INSTALL REAR AXLE SHAFT WITH PARKING BRAKE PLATE LH

  1. Install a new O-ring to the axle housing.

  1. Install the rear axle shaft with parking brake plate with the 4 nuts.

    Torque:
    60 N*m{ 612 kgf*cm , 44 ft.*lbf }



3. INSTALL PARKING BRAKE ASSEMBLY

  1. Install the parking brake assembly (Click here).



4. INSTALL REAR SPEED SENSOR LH

  1. Install the speed sensor with the nut.

    Torque:
    7.8 N*m{ 80 kgf*cm , 69 in.*lbf }

    NOTICE:
    1. Make sure there are no pieces of iron or other foreign matter attached to the sensor tip.
    2. While inserting the speed sensor into the axle hole, do not strike or damage the sensor tip.
    3. After installing the speed sensor, make sure there is no clearance or foreign matter between the sensor stay part and the axle.
    4. Make sure there is no foreign matter attached to the speed sensor rotor.

  1. Connect the speed sensor connector.

    NOTICE:
    Securely connect the connector.



5. INSTALL REAR FLEXIBLE HOSE LH

  1. Connect the rear flexible hose to the connecting point with the brake tube, and then install a new clip.

  1. Using a union nut wrench, connect the brake tube to the rear flexible hose while holding the rear flexible hose with a wrench.

    Torque:
    15 N*m{ 155 kgf*cm , 11 ft.*lbf }

    NOTICE:
    1. Do not bend or damage the brake tube.
    2. Do not allow any foreign matter such as dirt and dust to enter the brake tube from the connecting point.
    3. Use the formula to calculate special torque values for situations where a union nut wrench is combined with a torque wrench (Click here).

  1. Install the rear flexible hose and a new gasket to the rear disc brake cylinder with the union bolt.

    Torque:
    31 N*m{ 316 kgf*cm , 23 ft.*lbf }

    HINT:
    Install the rear flexible hose lock securely to the lock hole in the cylinder.



6. FILL RESERVOIR WITH BRAKE FLUID


7. BLEED BRAKE LINE

  1. Turn the engine switch on (IG).

  1. Remove the brake master cylinder reservoir filler cap assembly.

  1. Add brake fluid until the fluid level is between the MIN and MAX lines of the reservoir.

  1. Repeatedly depress the brake pedal and bleed air from the bleeder plug of the front disc brake cylinder RH.

  1. Repeat the step above until the air is completely bled, and then tighten the bleeder plug while depressing the brake pedal.

    Torque:
    11 N*m{ 110 kgf*cm , 8 ft.*lbf }

  1. Bleed the air from the bleeder plug of the front disc brake cylinder LH using the same procedure as for the RH side.

  1. With the brake pedal depressed, loosen the bleeder plug of the rear disc brake cylinder RH, continue to hold the brake pedal and allow brake fluid to be drained from the bleeder plug while the pump motor operates.

    HINT:
    1. Air is bled as the pump motor operates while the brake pedal is being depressed.
    2. Be sure to release the brake pedal to stop the motor after approximately 100 seconds of continuous operation.
    3. As brake fluid is continuously drained while the pump operates, it is not necessary to repeatedly depress the brake pedal.

  1. When there is no more air in the brake fluid, tighten the bleeder plug, and then release the brake pedal.

    Torque:
    11 N*m{ 110 kgf*cm , 8 ft.*lbf }

  1. Bleed the air from the bleeder plug of the rear disc brake cylinder LH using the same procedure as for the RH side.

  1. Turn the engine switch off.

  1. Inspect for brake fluid leaks.

  1. Check and adjust the brake fluid level (Click here).

  1. Clear the DTCs (Click here).



8. CHECK BRAKE FLUID LEVEL IN RESERVOIR



  1. Turn the engine switch off and fully depress the brake pedal 40 times or more to release the pressure in the accumulator.

    HINT:
    When the pressure in the accumulator is released, the pedal stroke will lengthen.

  1. Adjust the fluid level so that it is at the MAX line.

    HINT:
    When the engine switch is turned on (IG), brake fluid is sent to the accumulator and the fluid level decreases by approximately 10 mm (0.394 in.) from the MAX line.

    Brake Fluid:
    SAE J1703 or FMVSS NO. 116 DOT 3



9. INSPECT FOR BRAKE FLUID LEAK


10. INSTALL REAR WHEEL

Torque:
112 N*m{ 1137 kgf*cm , 82 ft.*lbf }



11. INSPECT PARKING BRAKE PEDAL TRAVEL

  1. Remove the lower instrument panel finish panel sub-assembly (Click here).

  1. Completely release the parking brake pedal.




  1. Loosen the lock nut and adjusting nut to completely release the parking brake cable.

  1. Temporarily install the hub nuts to the hub bolts.

    HINT:
    Securely install the hub nuts to the rear disc.




  1. Turn the shoe adjuster so that it expands until the disc locks.
    Text in Illustration 

    		Shoe Adjuster Contracts

    		Shoe Adjuster Expands

  1. Turn the shoe adjuster so that it contracts until the disc can rotate smoothly.

    Standard:
    8 notches

  1. Check that there is no brake drag against the shoe.




  1. Turn the adjusting nut until the parking brake pedal travel is corrected to be within the specified range.

    Standard parking brake pedal travel:
    5 to 7 clicks at 294 N (30 kgf, 66 lbf)

    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Adjusting Nut

  1. Using a wrench or an equivalent tool, hold the adjusting nut and tighten the lock nut.

    Torque:
    5.4 N*m{ 55 kgf*cm , 48 in.*lbf }

  1. Operate the parking brake pedal 3 to 4 times and check the parking brake pedal travel.

  1. Check that there is no brake drag against the shoe.




  1. Additionally, excessive pedal travel can be adjusted on the No. 1 parking brake cable as follows.

    1. Loosen the No. 1 parking brake cable lock nut.

    2. Turn the No. 1 parking brake cable adjustment nut until the parking brake pedal travel is correct.

      Standard parking brake pedal travel:
      5 to 7 clicks at 294 N (30 kgf, 66 lbf)

    3. Tighten the No. 1 parking brake cable lock nut.

      Torque:
      13 N*m{ 127 kgf*cm , 9 ft.*lbf }

      NOTICE:
      Pay attention that the adjustment nut does not rotate together with the lock nut during tightening.

    4. Depress and release the parking brake pedal 3 or 4 times. Then check the number of clicks when depressing the pedal.

      Standard:
      5 to 7 clicks

      If the result is not as specified, adjust the parking brake pedal travel again.

  1. Remove the hub nuts from the hub bolts.

  1. Install the lower instrument panel finish panel sub-assembly (Click here).

  1. When operating the parking brake lever, check that the brake warning light comes on.

    OK:
    The brake warning light always illuminates at the first click.



12. CHECK SPEED SENSOR SIGNAL

  1. Check the speed sensor signal (Click here).
