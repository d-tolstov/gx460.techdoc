DTC    P0550    Power Steering Pressure Sensor Circuit Malfunction

DTC    P0552    Power Steering Pressure Sensor Circuit Low Input

DTC    P0553    Power Steering Pressure Sensor Circuit High Input

for Preparation Click here


DESCRIPTION

The power steering oil pressure switch is turned on when a power steering wheel load is generated by turning the steering wheel. The ECM regulates the engine idling speed according to the voltage output of the sensor.

DTC No.
DTC Detection Condition
Trouble Area
P0550
Power steering oil pressure switch voltage is below 0.28 V, or higher than 4.9 V for 0.5 seconds while the engine is running (1 trip detection logic).
  1. Open or short in power steering oil pressure switch circuit
  2. Power steering oil pressure switch
  3. ECM
P0552
Power steering oil pressure switch voltage is below 0.28 V for 0.5 seconds while the engine is running (1 trip detection logic).
  1. Open or short in power steering oil pressure switch circuit
  2. Power steering oil pressure switch
  3. ECM
P0553
Power steering oil pressure switch voltage is higher than 4.9 V for 0.5 seconds while the engine is running (1 trip detection logic).
  1. Open or short in power steering oil pressure switch circuit
  2. Power steering oil pressure switch
  3. ECM

WIRING DIAGRAM




MONITOR DESCRIPTION

The ECM monitors the switch voltage and uses this value to regulate the engine idling speed. When the sensor output voltage deviates from the normal operating range, the ECM determines that there is a malfunction in the power steering oil pressure switch and stores a DTC.

MONITOR STRATEGY


Frequency of operation
Continuous

CONFIRMATION DRIVING PATTERN

  1. Idle the engine for 5 seconds or more.

INSPECTION PROCEDURE

HINT:
  1. If other DTCs related to different systems that have terminal E2 as the ground terminal are stored simultaneously, there may be an open circuit between terminal E2 and body ground.
  2. Read freeze frame data using the intelligent tester. Freeze frame data records the engine condition when malfunctions are detected. When troubleshooting, freeze frame data can help determine if the vehicle was moving or stationary, if the engine was warmed up or not, if the air-fuel ratio was lean or rich, and other data from the time the malfunction occurred.
