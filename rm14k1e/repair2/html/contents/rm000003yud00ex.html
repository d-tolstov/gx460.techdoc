1.WARNING NOTIFICATION FUNCTION

NOTICE:
  1. Before performing inspections or repairs which may cause warning messages to occur or DTCs to be stored, such as a simulation test or road test, it is necessary to activate warning notification restraint mode so that the G-BOOK center does not mistake the warnings for indications of real problems.
  2. Warning notification restraint mode can be entered by using the intelligent tester or operating the display and navigation module display.

HINT:
The warning notification function sends a warning message signal, received from the combination meter assembly via CAN communication, to the G-BOOK center using the G-BOOK device if a warning is displayed due to a vehicle malfunction.

  1. Warning notification restraint mode

    HINT:
    1. Performing the following procedure will cause the vehicle to enter warning notification restraint mode.
    2. After warning notification restraint mode is entered, driving the vehicle 10 km (6 miles) will cancel the mode.


    1. Enter warning notification restraint mode (using the intelligent tester).

      1. Turn the engine switch off.
      2. Connect the intelligent tester to the DLC3.
      3. Start the engine.
      4. Enter the following menus: Powertrain / Engine / DTC.

    2. Enter warning notification restraint mode (using the display and navigation module display).

      1. Enter diagnostic mode of the navigation system (for HDD) (Click here).


2.STRICT PROHIBITION OF MANUAL EMERGENCY CALL USING EMERGENCY CALL SERVICE

  1. Do not make an emergency call when not in emergency situations. For inspections, use the manual maintenance check.

    NOTICE:
    1. If the emergency call service is used to connect to the G-BOOK center when not in an emergency situation, an unnecessary workload will be generated at the G-BOOK center. This may cause a delayed response by the G-BOOK center to other emergency calls.
    2. If emergency vehicles are dispatched due to an accidental operation of the emergency call switch, you may be charged for the dispatch of the vehicle or punished according to related laws.

  2. If an accidental operation of the emergency call switch is made:

    1. If an accidental operation of the emergency call switch is made, contact the G-BOOK center to inform the center that the call was made by mistake.

    2. The emergency call cannot be halted or finished from the vehicle. Only the G-BOOK center can halt or finish the call.

    3. If the G-BOOK center cannot be informed that the call was made by mistake, the center will report to authorities, emergency facilities and dealerships in compliance with its agreements. As a result, emergency vehicles may be dispatched.


3.PRECAUTIONS FOR G-BOOK

  1. After replacing the display and navigation module display or the telematics transceiver and performing the vehicle contract setting, perform the procedure to resume the G-BOOK service and to start the emergency call service (Click here).

  2. The display and navigation module display functions as the G-BOOK device and has the G-BOOK ID.


4.INSPECTION PROCEDURE FOR TELEMATICS TRANSCEIVER (DATA COMMUNICATION MODULE [DCM])

  1. The telematics transceiver uses a CDMA2000 1X type radio wave signal. The radio signals and communication lines may occasionally be unstable depending on factors such as time or location. Therefore, network access errors may occur intermittently because of radio signal conditions. To determine if there is a problem, it may be necessary to make a judgment based on frequency of the phenomenon or by making a comparison with a comparable type of cell phone.

  2. The display and navigation module display and the telematics transceiver have a one-to-one relationship in the registered G-BOOK system. Therefore, if the display and navigation module display or the telematics transceiver is replaced, the network cannot be accessed unless the vehicle contract setting is performed. Use of a substitute component to confirm network access is also not possible.

  3. It is not permitted to disassemble the telematics transceiver (the case cannot be opened). If the telematics transceiver malfunctions, replace it.


5.PRECAUTIONS FOR EMERGENCY CALL SERVICE

  1. Depending on the content of troubleshooting, a manual maintenance check may need to be performed.

    HINT:
    Communication charges are not incurred for the manual maintenance check because the telematics transceiver is used for communication (the communication cost is included in the G-BOOK service cost).

  2. The telematics transceiver functions as the emergency call service device and has the mayday ID.


6.AUTOMATIC MAINTENANCE CHECK FOR EMERGENCY CALL SERVICE

  1. The emergency call system regularly performs an automatic maintenance check and communication test with the G-BOOK center so that the emergency call service can always function correctly. There are no operations required to initiate an automatic maintenance check. The automatic maintenance check is performed as follows:

    1. The automatic maintenance check ends 1 to 2 minutes after it starts.
    2. During the automatic maintenance check, a telephone call or network access is not possible using the G-BOOK system. In this case, wait several minutes before trying again.
    3. If communication is interrupted for some reason after the automatic maintenance check has started, the automatic maintenance check will resume the next time the engine switch is turned on (ACC).
    4. There are no communication charges incurred for the manual and automatic maintenance checks because the telematics transceiver is used for communication (the communication cost is included in the G-BOOK service cost).


7.IF EMERGENCY CALL CANNOT BE MADE

  1. The emergency call service may not be available, or its quality may deteriorate in the following cases (for details, refer to the terms of service and the operation manual for the device).

    Cause
    		Detail
    Communication is interrupted due to malfunctions in the G-BOOK device or any other related device.
    		The G-BOOK device does not operate normally, such as when the device is damaged or malfunctioning due to an impact caused by a collision.
    Communication is interrupted due to a G-BOOK center system error.
    1. The G-BOOK center system may need either periodic or urgent maintenance.
    2. An extreme volume of emergency calls is made to the G-BOOK center due to a large-scale traffic accident or fire.
    3. Malfunctions or problems with the G-BOOK center system.
    4. The G-BOOK center system is damaged due to a fire.
    5. The emergency call service is suspended or halted due to emergency call provider reasons.
    Communication is interrupted due to the network used for the emergency call system.*
    1. The registered vehicle is located outside the cellular phone service area used for the emergency call system.
    2. The vehicle is overseas.
    3. The vehicle is in a cellular phone service area where communication is not possible, such as an indoor environment, a basement parking area, mountains, tunnels or beside a building.
    4. Network availability or the time of use is limited, such as when cellular phone networks or public fixed networks (including junction networks) are extremely busy.
    5. Network availability is limited by the communication service provider due to a large-scale disaster.
    6. The cellular phone network or public fixed network (including junction networks) service used for the emergency call system is suspended or halted due to accidents, maintenance or construction.
    The G-BOOK center system cannot determine the vehicle location.
    1. The vehicle is in an area where Global Positioning System (GPS) radio waves cannot be received or where it is difficult for radio waves to penetrate, such as an indoor environment, a basement parking area, mountains, tunnels or beside a building.
    2. The vehicle location cannot be determined normally or accurately because the vehicle is in one of the following areas:
      1. Areas that cause a large error in the location calculation using the GPS, such as in tunnels or a densely built-up area, or after getting off a ferry.
      1. Areas that cause a large error in map matching due to an old map database.
    3. The GPS system is malfunctioning.
    Others
    1. Although a manual call is made, the situation cannot be confirmed because the user cannot respond to questions from the G-BOOK center.
    2. The G-BOOK contract has not been established, the user fell behind in payments or canceled the service.
    3. The emergency call service start procedure has not been completed.

    NOTICE:
    *: Even when the vehicle is within the cellular phone service area, it may be difficult to make emergency or normal phone calls due to radio wave signal conditions or busy lines. In this case, it may not be possible to access the G-BOOK center. As a result, no calls can be made to ask for rescue in the event of a traffic accident. If emergency calls cannot be made, use a landline telephone or cellular phone.


8.ALARM NOTIFICATION FUNCTION

  1. If the vehicle automatic alarm is activated, the G-BOOK center makes a phone call to the customer.

    NOTICE:
    1. When servicing the vehicle, make sure that the vehicle does not experience any conditions that cause a telephone call to be made by a G-BOOK operator.
    2. Before performing inspections or repairs which may activate the alarm notification function, be sure to inform the customer that they may receive a telephone call from a G-BOOK operator.

    HINT:
    Customers can cancel the alarm notification on the display and navigation module display.
