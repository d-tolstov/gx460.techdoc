VEHICLE STABILITY CONTROL SYSTEM > CALIBRATION

for Preparation Click here



DESCRIPTION

  1. After replacing VSC-related components, clearing and reading the sensor calibration data is necessary.

  2. Follow the chart to perform calibration.

    Part Replaced
    		Necessary Operation
    Master Cylinder Solenoid (Skid Control ECU)
    1. Clearing zero point calibration data
    2. Yaw rate and acceleration sensor zero point calibration
    3. Steering angle sensor zero point calibration
    4. Downhill assist control calibration (w/ Downhill Assist Control)
    5. Crawl control calibration (w/ Crawl Control)
    Yaw Rate and Acceleration Sensor
    1. Clearing zero point calibration data
    2. Yaw rate and acceleration sensor zero point calibration
    3. Steering angle sensor zero point calibration
    Spiral Cable Sub-assembly (Steering Angle Sensor)
    1. Clearing zero point calibration data
    2. Yaw rate and acceleration sensor zero point calibration
    3. Steering angle sensor zero point calibration


PERFORM YAW RATE AND ACCELERATION SENSOR AND STEERING ANGLE SENSOR ZERO POINT CALIBRATION (When Using Intelligent Tester)

NOTICE:
  1. While obtaining the zero points, keep the vehicle stationary and do not vibrate, tilt, move, or shake it (do not start the engine).
  2. Be sure to perform this procedure on a level surface (with an inclination of less than 1%).

  1. Clear the zero point calibration data.

    1. Turn the engine switch off.

    2. Check that the steering wheel is centered.

    3. Check that the shift lever is in P.

    4. Connect the intelligent tester to the DLC3.

    5. Turn the engine switch on (IG).

    6. Turn the intelligent tester on.

    7. Enter the following menus: Chassis / ABS/VSC/TRC / Utility / Reset Memory.

    8. Select the skid control ECU to clear the zero point calibration data using the intelligent tester.

    9. Turn the engine switch off.

  2. Perform zero point calibration of the yaw rate and acceleration sensor.

    1. Turn the engine switch off.

    2. Check that the steering wheel is centered.

    3. Check that the shift lever is in P.

      NOTICE:
      1. DTCs C1210 (Zero Point Calibration of Yaw Rate Sensor Undone) and C1336 (Zero Point Calibration of Acceleration Sensor Undone) are stored if the shift lever is not in P.
      2. If a DTC is output that indicates zero point calibration is incomplete, repeat the procedure starting at the step for clearing the zero point calibration data and system information.

    4. Connect the intelligent tester to the DLC3.

    5. Turn the engine switch on (IG).

    6. Turn the intelligent tester on.

    7. Enter the following menus: Chassis / ABS/VSC/TRC / Utility / Test Mode.

    8. Keep the vehicle stationary on a level surface for 5 seconds or more.

    9. Check that the slip indicator light comes on for several seconds and then blinks in the test mode pattern (0.125 seconds on and 0.125 seconds off).

      HINT:
      1. If the slip indicator light does not blink, perform zero point calibration again.
      2. The zero point calibration is performed only once after the system enters test mode.
      3. Calibration cannot be performed again until the stored data is cleared.

    10. Turn the engine switch off and disconnect the intelligent tester.

  3. Drive the vehicle straight ahead at 40 km/h (25 mph) or more for at least 10 seconds.


PERFORM YAW RATE AND ACCELERATION SENSOR AND STEERING ANGLE SENSOR ZERO POINT CALIBRATION (When Using SST Check Wire)

NOTICE:
  1. While obtaining the zero points, keep the vehicle stationary and do not vibrate, tilt, move, or shake it (do not start the engine).
  2. Be sure to perform this procedure on a level surface (with an inclination of less than 1%).



  1. Clear the zero point calibration data.

    1. Turn the engine switch off.

    2. Check that the steering wheel is centered.

    3. Check that the shift lever is in P.

    4. Turn the engine switch on (IG).

    5. The ABS warning light and slip indicator light come on for 3 seconds to indicate that the initial check is completed.




    6. Using SST, connect and disconnect terminals 12 (TS) and 4 (CG) of the DLC3 4 times or more within 8 seconds.

    7. Check that the slip indicator light comes on.



  2. Perform zero point calibration of the yaw rate and acceleration sensor.

    1. Turn the engine switch off.

    2. Check that the steering wheel is centered.

    3. Check that the shift lever is in P.

      NOTICE:
      1. DTCs 36 (Zero Point Calibration of Yaw Rate Sensor Undone) and 39 (Zero Point Calibration of Acceleration Sensor Undone) are stored if the shift lever is not in P.
      2. If a DTC is output that indicates zero point calibration is incomplete, repeat the procedure starting at the step for clearing the zero point calibration data and system information.




    4. Using SST, connect terminals 12 (TS) and 4 (CG) of the DLC3.

      SST

    5. Turn the engine switch on (IG).

    6. Keep the vehicle stationary on a level surface for 5 seconds or more.

    7. Check that the slip indicator light comes on for several seconds and then blinks in the test mode pattern (0.125 seconds on and 0.125 seconds off).

      HINT:
      1. If the slip indicator light does not blink, perform zero point calibration again.
      2. The zero point calibration is performed only once after the system enters test mode.
      3. Calibration cannot be performed again until the stored data is cleared.

    8. Turn the engine switch off and disconnect SST from the DLC3.

  3. Drive the vehicle straight ahead at 40 km/h (25 mph) or more for at least 10 seconds.


PERFORM DOWNHILL ASSIST CONTROL CALIBRATION (w/ Downhill Assist Control)

  1. Enter test mode (when using the intelligent tester).

    1. Turn the engine switch off.

    2. Connect the intelligent tester to the DLC3.

    3. Turn the engine switch on (IG).

    4. Turn the intelligent tester on.

    5. Enter the following menus: Chassis / ABS/VSC/TRC / Utility / Test Mode.



  2. Enter test mode (when using SST check wire).

    1. Turn the engine switch off.




    2. Using SST, connect terminals 12 (TS) and 4 (CG) of the DLC3.

      SST
      09843-18040  
      Text in Illustration 
      *a
      		Front view of DLC3

  3. Turn the downhill assist control switch off.

  4. Push the downhill assist control switch and check that the downhill assist control indicator light is blinking.

  5. Turn the downhill assist control switch off.

  6. Turn the engine switch off.

  7. Check if DTC C120A is output.

    HINT:
    If DTC C120A is not output, calibration was performed successfully.


PERFORM CRAWL CONTROL CALIBRATION (w/ Crawl Control)

  1. Enter test mode (when using the intelligent tester).

    1. Turn the engine switch off.

    2. Connect the intelligent tester to the DLC3.

    3. Turn the engine switch on (IG).

    4. Turn the intelligent tester on.

    5. Enter the following menus: Chassis / ABS/VSC/TRC / Utility / Test Mode.



  2. Enter test mode (when using SST check wire).

    1. Turn the engine switch off.




    2. Using SST, connect terminals 12 (TS) and 4 (CG) of the DLC3.

      SST
      09843-18040  
      Text in Illustration 
      *a
      		Front view of DLC3

  3. Push the ON/OFF switch and check that the crawl indicator light is on while the switch is being pushed.




  4. Turn the ON/OFF switch off.

    *2

  5. Turn the engine switch off.

  6. Check if DTC C120A is output.

    HINT:
    If DTC C120A is not output, calibration was performed successfully.


PROCEDURES NECESSARY WHEN CABLE IS DISCONNECTED/RECONNECTED TO BATTERY TERMINAL

NOTICE:
The steering angle display on the combination meter does not appear if any of the following is performed: 1) The cable is disconnected and reconnected to the negative (-) battery terminal, 2) the steering angle sensor connector is disconnected, or 3) a fuse related to the steering angle sensor is removed.

  1. Drive the vehicle straight ahead at 40 km/h (25 mph) or more for at least 10 seconds.

  2. Confirm the steering angle display function.

    1. Turn the steering wheel to the left and right and confirm that the steering angle display function is normal.
