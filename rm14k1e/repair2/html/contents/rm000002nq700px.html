DTC    B2000    Short to B+ in Seat Belt Motor RH Circuit

for Preparation Click here


DESCRIPTION

The seat belt control ECU receives crash information from the driving support ECU and skid control ECU via CAN communication and tightens the seat belt by operating the motor inside the front seat outer belt RH.
When DTC B2000 is stored, DTCs B2016, B2026, B2027 and B2035 are not stored.

DTC Code
DTC Detection Condition
Trouble Area
B2000
During the initial check or with the engine switch on (IG), the seat belt motor RH circuit has a power supply short (the voltage at terminals MOR+ and MOR- is 8.5 V or higher) for 1 second or more.
  1. Front seat outer belt assembly RH
  2. Harness or connector
  3. Seat belt control ECU

INSPECTION PROCEDURE

CAUTION:
The pretensioner is built into the front seat outer belt assembly RH. Be sure to follow the correct inspection procedure, as failure to follow the correct procedure (such as inspection of incorrect connectors) may activate the pretensioner.

1.CHECK FRONT SEAT OUTER BELT ASSEMBLY RH CIRCUIT



  1. Disconnect the G34 ECU connector.

  1. Measure the voltage according to the value(s) in the table below.

    Standard Voltage:
    Tester Connection
    		Switch Condition
    		Specified Condition

    		Engine switch on (IG)
    		Below 1 V
