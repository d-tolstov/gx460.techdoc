DTC    B2007    Open in Seat Belt Motor LH Circuit

for Preparation Click here


DESCRIPTION

The seat belt control ECU receives crash information from the driving support ECU and skid control ECU via CAN communication and tightens the seat belt by operating the motor inside the front seat outer belt LH.
When DTC B2007 is stored, DTCs B2031 and B2032 are not stored.

DTC Code
DTC Detection Condition
Trouble Area
B2007
During the initial check or with the engine switch on (IG), the seat belt motor LH circuit has an open (the voltage at terminal MOL+ is 8.5 V or higher and the voltage at terminal MOL- is below 2 V) for 1 second or more.
  1. Front seat outer belt assembly LH
  2. Harness or connector
  3. Seat belt control ECU

INSPECTION PROCEDURE

CAUTION:
The pretensioner is built into the front seat outer belt assembly LH. Be sure to follow the correct inspection procedure, as failure to follow the correct procedure (such as inspection of incorrect connectors) may activate the pretensioner.

1.CHECK FRONT SEAT OUTER BELT ASSEMBLY LH CIRCUIT



  1. Disconnect the G34 ECU connector.

  1. Measure the resistance according to the value(s) in the table below.

    Standard Resistance:
    Tester Connection
    		Condition
    		Specified Condition
    G34-3 (MOL+) - G34-4 (MOL-)
    		Always
    		Below 20 Ω

  1. Disconnect the R4 belt connector.

  1. Using a service wire, connect terminals R4-1 (MOL+) and R4-2 (MOL-) of the connector of the front seat outer belt LH.
