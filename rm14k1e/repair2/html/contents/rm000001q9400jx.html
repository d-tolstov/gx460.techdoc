REAR BRAKE > DISASSEMBLY

for Preparation Click here



1. REMOVE CYLINDER BOOT



  1. Using a screwdriver, remove the cylinder boot from the rear disc brake cylinder assembly.

    HINT:
    Tape the screwdriver tip before use.

    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Protective Tape



2. REMOVE REAR DISC BRAKE PISTON



  1. Place a piece of cloth or a similar object between the rear disc brake piston and rear disc brake cylinder.

  1. Use compressed air to remove the rear disc brake piston from the rear disc brake cylinder.

    CAUTION:
    Do not place your fingers in front of the piston when using compressed air.

    NOTICE:
    Do not spatter the brake fluid.



3. REMOVE PISTON SEAL



  1. Using a screwdriver, remove the piston seal from the rear disc brake cylinder.

    HINT:
    Tape the screwdriver tip before use.

    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Protective Tape

    NOTICE:
    Do not damage the inner cylinder or cylinder groove.



4. REMOVE REAR DISC BRAKE BLEEDER PLUG CAP


5. REMOVE REAR DISC BRAKE BLEEDER PLUG

  1. Remove the bleeder plug cap and bleeder plug from the rear disc brake cylinder assembly.
