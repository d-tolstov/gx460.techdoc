4. INSTALL WINDSHIELD GLASS ADHESIVE DAM

  1. Apply Primer G to the glass where the new adhesive dam will be installed.

5. INSTALL WINDSHIELD GLASS



  1. Position the glass.

    1. Using suction cups, place the glass in the correct position.

    2. Check that the entire contact surface of the glass rim is perfectly even.

    3. Place matchmarks on the glass and vehicle body at the locations indicated in the illustration.

      HINT:
      1. Placing matchmarks is only necessary when installing new glass. When reusing the glass, matchmarks should already be present.
      2. When reusing the glass, check and correct the matchmark positions.

      NOTICE:
      Check that the stoppers are attached to the vehicle body correctly.

    4. Using suction cups, remove the glass.
      Text in Illustration 
      *a
      		Matchmark




  1. Using a brush, apply Primer M to the exposed part of the vehicle body.

    NOTICE:
    1. Allow the primer to dry for 3 minutes or more.
    2. Do not apply primer to the adhesive.
    3. Throw away any leftover primer.
    4. Do not apply too much primer.

    Text in Illustration

    		Primer M
    *2
    		Adhesive
    *a
    		CORRECT
    *b
    		INCORRECT



  1. Using a brush or sponge, apply Primer G to the contact surface of the glass.




    Text in Illustration

    HINT:
    If the primer is applied to an area that is not specified, apply non-residue solvent to a clean cloth and wipe off the excess primer before it dries.

    NOTICE:
    1. Allow the primer to dry for 3 minutes or more.
    2. Throw away any leftover primer.
    3. Do not apply too much primer.



  1. Apply adhesive to the glass.

    Adhesive:
    Toyota Genuine Windshield Glass Adhesive or equivalent


    1. Cut off the tip of a cartridge nozzle as shown in the illustration.

    3. Apply adhesive to the glass as shown in the illustration.

  1. Install the glass to the vehicle body.

    1. Using suction cups, position the glass so that the matchmarks are aligned. Press it in gently along the rim.

    2. Lightly press the outer surface of the glass to ensure that it is securely installed to the vehicle body.

      NOTICE:
      1. Check that the stoppers are attached to the vehicle body correctly.
      2. Check that the vehicle body and glass have a small gap between them.

    3. Hold the glass in place securely with protective tape or equivalent until the adhesive hardens.

      NOTICE:
      Do not drive the vehicle within the time written in the table below.

      Minimum Time:
      Temperature
      		Minimum Time Prior to Driving Vehicle
      35°C (95°F)
      		1 hour 30 minutes
      20°C (68°F)
      		5 hours
      5°C (41°F)
      		24 hours

      		Matchmark

  1. w/ Windshield Deicer System:
    Connect the windshield deicer connector.

  1. Seal any leaks with auto glass sealer.

11. INSTALL RAIN SENSOR

  1. Clean the windshield glass with a piece of cloth, etc.

    NOTICE:
    1. Make sure that there is no rain sensor tape residue remaining on the windshield glass. If there is, remove the residue.
    2. If there is any rain sensor tape residue remaining on the windshield glass, replace the tape.



  1. When installing a new rain sensor or replacing the rain sensor tape:

    1. Peel off the release sheet.

      HINT:
      Do not touch the silicon coated surfaces.




    2. Attach the 2 guides, and then carefully install the rain sensor to the glass surface so that there are no air bubbles from forming between the contact surfaces.
      Text in Illustration 
      *1
      		Stopper
      *2
      		Guide

      NOTICE:
      Do not touch the silicon coated or glass surfaces.

    3. Push in the stopper.

    4. Connect the connector.

  1. Insert the mandrel part of a new No. 3 windshield outside moulding clip into the nose piece.




  1. Using the riveter, install the No. 3 windshield outside moulding clips as shown in the illustration.

    HINT:
