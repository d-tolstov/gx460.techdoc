FRONT BRAKE > REMOVAL

for Preparation Click here


HINT:
  1. Use the same procedure for the RH and LH sides.
  2. The procedure listed below is for the LH side.



1. REMOVE FRONT WHEEL


2. DRAIN BRAKE FLUID

NOTICE:
Wash off the brake fluid immediately if it comes into contact with a painted surface.



3. REMOVE FRONT DISC BRAKE PAD



  1. Remove the pin hold clip.
    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Hole Pin
    *2
    		Pin Hold Clip
    *3
    		Anti-rattle Spring

    NOTICE:
    The pin hold clip can be reused if it has sufficient rebound; no deformation or wear; and has had all rust, dirt and foreign matter cleaned off.

  1. Remove the 2 hole pins.




  1. Remove the front disc brake anti-rattle spring.

    NOTICE:
    The anti-rattle spring can be reused if it has sufficient rebound; no deformation, cracks or wear; and has had all rust, dirt and foreign matter cleaned off.




  1. Remove the 2 front disc brake pads from the disc brake cylinder.

  1. Remove the front No. 1 anti-squeal shims from each pad.



4. REMOVE DISC BRAKE CYLINDER ASSEMBLY LH



  1. Using a union nut wrench, disconnect the brake tube from the disc brake cylinder assembly.

    HINT:
    Use a container to catch brake fluid as it drains out.




  1. Remove the 2 bolts and disc brake cylinder assembly.



5. REMOVE FRONT DISC



  1. Place matchmarks on the disc and axle hub if planning to reuse the disc.

    		Matchmark

  1. Remove the front disc.
