1. INSTALL RACK AND PINION POWER STEERING GEAR ASSEMBLY

  1. Install the steering gear assembly with the 2 bolts, 2 washers and 2 nuts.

    Torque:
    120 N*m{ 1224 kgf*cm , 89 ft.*lbf }

    HINT:
    Hold the bolts and tighten the nuts to install the steering gear assembly.



2. CONNECT PRESSURE FEED TUBE ASSEMBLY

  1. Install the pressure feed tube clamp with the 2 bolts.

    Torque:
    28 N*m{ 286 kgf*cm , 21 ft.*lbf }

  1. Connect the pressure feed tube (return tube side) to the steering gear with the clamp.

  1. Using a union nut wrench, connect the pressure feed tube (pressure feed tube side) to the steering gear and connect the tube to the vane pump.

    Torque:
    25 N*m{ 255 kgf*cm , 18 ft.*lbf }

    HINT:
    Use the formula to calculate special torque values for situations where a union nut wrench is combined with a torque wrench (Click here).



3. CONNECT TIE ROD END SUB-ASSEMBLY LH

  1. Connect the tie rod end to the steering knuckle with the nut.

    Torque:
    91 N*m{ 928 kgf*cm , 67 ft.*lbf }

    NOTICE:
    Tighten the nut up to an additional 60° if the holes for the cotter pin are not aligned.

  1. Install a new cotter pin.



4. CONNECT TIE ROD END SUB-ASSEMBLY RH

HINT:
Use the same procedure described for the LH side.

7. ADD POWER STEERING FLUID


8. BLEED POWER STEERING FLUID

  1. Check the fluid level.

  1. Jack up the front of the vehicle and support it with stands.

  1. Turn the steering wheel.

    1. With the engine stopped, turn the wheel slowly from lock to lock several times.

  1. Lower the vehicle.

  1. Start the engine.

    1. Run the engine at idle for a few minutes.

  1. Turn the steering wheel.

    1. With the engine idling, turn the wheel to the left or right full lock position and keep it there for 2 to 3 seconds, then turn the wheel to the opposite full lock position and keep it there for 2 to 3 seconds.*1

    2. Repeat *1 several times.

  1. Stop the engine.




  1. Check for foaming or emulsification.
