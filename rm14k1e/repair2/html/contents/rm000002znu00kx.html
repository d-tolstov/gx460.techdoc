NOTICE:
  1. Avoid touching the stroke portion of the rod as much as possible to prevent foreign matter from adhering to it. Be sure to hold the cylinders while performing this procedure.
  2. Do not wear cotton gloves or other similar materials when handling the rod. Fibers may attach to the rod and result in gas leaks.
  3. Do not apply any load to the cylinders in the horizontal direction in order to prevent the rod from being deformed.

HINT:
  1. Use the same procedure for the RH and LH sides.
  2. The procedure listed below is for the LH side.



1. INSTALL BACK DOOR STAY BOLT

  1. When replacing the back door stay bolt with a new one:

    1. Clean the threaded portion on the vehicle body with non-residue solvent.

    2. Install the back door stay bolt.

      Torque:
      22 N*m{ 224 kgf*cm , 16 ft.*lbf }

  1. When reusing the back door stay bolt:

    1. Clean the threaded portion on the vehicle body with non-residue solvent.

    2. Apply adhesive to the threads of the back door stay bolt.

      Adhesive:
      Toyota Genuine Adhesive 1324, Three Bond 1324 or equivalent

    3. Install the back door stay bolt.

      Torque:
      22 N*m{ 224 kgf*cm , 16 ft.*lbf }



2. INSTALL BACK WINDOW STAY ASSEMBLY LH

  1. Install the 2 stop rings to the back door stay assembly.




  1. Install the back window stay assembly.

    CAUTION:
    Install the back window stay while supporting the back door glass by hand.

  1. Check that the back window stay is attached to the ball joint and cannot be pulled off.
