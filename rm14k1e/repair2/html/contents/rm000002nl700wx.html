DTC    B1920/77    Short in Rear Pretensioner Squib RH Circuit

DTC    B1921/77    Open in Rear Pretensioner Squib RH Circuit

DTC    B1922/77    Short to GND in Rear Pretensioner Squib RH Circuit

DTC    B1923/77    Short to B+ in Rear Pretensioner Squib RH Circuit

for Preparation Click here


DESCRIPTION

The rear pretensioner squib RH circuit consists of the center airbag sensor and the rear No. 1 seat outer belt RH.
This circuit instructs the SRS to deploy when deployment conditions are met.
These DTCs are stored when a malfunction is detected in the rear pretensioner squib RH circuit.

DTC Code
DTC Detection Condition
Trouble Area
B1920/77
One of the following conditions is met:
  1. The center airbag sensor detects a line short circuit signal 5 times in the rear pretensioner squib RH circuit during the primary check.
  2. A rear pretensioner squib RH malfunction.
  3. A center airbag sensor malfunction.
  1. Floor wire
  2. Rear No. 1 seat outer belt assembly RH (Rear pretensioner squib RH)
  3. Center airbag sensor assembly
B1921/77
One of the following conditions is met:
  1. The center airbag sensor detects an open circuit signal in the rear pretensioner squib RH circuit for 2 seconds.
  2. A rear pretensioner squib RH malfunction.
  3. A center airbag sensor malfunction.
  1. Floor wire
  2. Rear No. 1 seat outer belt assembly RH (Rear pretensioner squib RH)
  3. Center airbag sensor assembly
B1922/77
One of the following conditions is met:
  1. The center airbag sensor detects a short circuit to ground signal in the rear pretensioner squib RH circuit for 0.5 seconds.
  2. A rear pretensioner squib RH malfunction.
  3. A center airbag sensor malfunction.
  1. Floor wire
  2. Rear No. 1 seat outer belt assembly RH (Rear pretensioner squib RH)
  3. Center airbag sensor assembly
B1923/77
One of the following conditions is met:
  1. The center airbag sensor detects a short circuit to B+ signal in the rear pretensioner squib RH circuit for 0.5 seconds.
  2. A rear pretensioner squib RH malfunction.
  3. A center airbag sensor malfunction.
  1. Floor wire
  2. Rear No. 1 seat outer belt assembly RH (Rear pretensioner squib RH)
  3. Center airbag sensor assembly

1.CHECK REAR NO. 1 SEAT OUTER BELT RH (REAR PRETENSIONER SQUIB RH)

  1. Turn the engine switch off.

  1. Disconnect the cable from the negative (-) battery terminal, and wait for at least 90 seconds.

  1. Disconnect the connectors from the rear No. 1 seat outer belt RH.

  1. Connect the white wire side of SST (resistance: 2.1 Î©) to connector C.

    CAUTION:
    Never connect the tester to the rear No. 1 seat outer belt RH (rear pretensioner squib RH) for measurement, as this may lead to a serious injury due to airbag deployment.

    NOTICE:
    1. Do not forcibly insert SST into the terminals of the connector when connecting SST.
    2. Insert SST straight into the terminals of the connector.
