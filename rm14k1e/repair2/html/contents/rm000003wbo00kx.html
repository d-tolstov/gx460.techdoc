1. DISCONNECT CABLE FROM NEGATIVE BATTERY TERMINAL

NOTICE:
  1. w/ Navigation System (for HDD):
    After the engine switch is turned off, the display and navigation module display (HDD navigation system) records various types of memory and settings. As a result, after turning the engine switch off, be sure to wait for the time specified in the following table before disconnecting the cable from the negative (-) battery terminal.

    Waiting Time before Disconnecting Cable from Negative (-) Battery Terminal:

    Condition
    		Waiting Time
    Vehicle enrolled in G-BOOK system
    		6 minutes
    Vehicle not enrolled in G-BOOK system
    		1 minute

  2. When disconnecting the cable, some systems need to be initialized after the cable is reconnected (Click here).



2. REMOVE DOOR SCUFF PLATE ASSEMBLY LH



  1. Put protective tape around the door scuff plate.
    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Protective Tape

  1. Using a screwdriver, detach the 4 clips, 10 claws and 2 guides.

    HINT:
    Tape the screwdriver tip before use.

  1. Disconnect the connector and remove the door scuff plate.



3. REMOVE COWL SIDE TRIM BOARD LH



  1. Remove the clip.

  1. Detach the clip and claw and remove the cowl side trim board.



4. REMOVE NO. 1 INSTRUMENT PANEL UNDER COVER SUB-ASSEMBLY



  1. Remove the screw.

  1. Detach the 2 clips and 2 guides.

  1. Disconnect the connector, detach the clamp and remove the No. 1 instrument panel under cover.



5. REMOVE FRONT NO. 1 CONSOLE BOX INSERT



  1. Detach the 2 clips and guide and remove the front No. 1 console box insert.



6. REMOVE INSTRUMENT PANEL FINISH PANEL END LH



  1. Detach the 11 clips and remove the instrument panel finish panel end.



7. REMOVE INSTRUMENT SIDE PANEL LH



  1. Put protective tape around the instrument side panel.
    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Protective Tape

  1. Using a moulding remover, detach the 3 clips and 2 guides and remove the instrument side panel.



8. REMOVE NO. 2 SWITCH HOLE BASE



  1. Put protective tape around the No. 2 switch hole base.
    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Protective Tape

  1. Using a moulding remover, detach the 5 clips and claw.

  1. Disconnect each connector and remove the No. 2 switch hole base.



9. REMOVE NO. 1 INSTRUMENT PANEL FINISH CUSHION



  1. Put protective tape around the No. 1 instrument panel finish cushion.
    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Protective Tape

  1. Using a moulding remover, detach the 4 clips.

  1. Disconnect the connector and remove the No. 1 instrument panel finish cushion.



10. REMOVE LOWER INSTRUMENT PANEL FINISH PANEL ASSEMBLY



  1. Detach the 4 clips.

  1. Disconnect each connector and cooler thermistor and remove the instrument panel finish panel.



11. REMOVE NO. 1 INSTRUMENT PANEL REGISTER ASSEMBLY



  1. Detach the 5 clips and remove the No. 1 instrument panel register.



12. REMOVE LOWER INSTRUMENT PANEL FINISH PANEL SUB-ASSEMBLY



  1. Using a screwdriver, detach the 2 claws and open the cover.

    HINT:
    Tape the screwdriver tip before use.

    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Protective Tape




  1. Remove the 2 bolts <C>.

  1. Detach the 13 clips and 2 guides.

  1. Disconnect each connector and cable and remove the lower instrument panel finish panel.



13. REMOVE LOWER NO. 1 INSTRUMENT PANEL AIRBAG ASSEMBLY



  1. Remove the 4 bolts and airbag assembly.

  1. Disconnect the connector.

    NOTICE:
    When handling the airbag connector, take care not to damage the airbag wire harness.



14. REMOVE DRIVER SIDE JUNCTION BLOCK ASSEMBLY



  1. Disconnect the 3 connectors.




  1. Detach the 2 claws and disconnect the 2 connectors labeled (1) as shown in the illustration.

  1. Disconnect the connector labeled (2).




  1. Remove the bolt and 2 nuts and disconnect the driver side junction block assembly.




  1. Detach the claw and disconnect the connector as shown in the illustration.




  1. Detach the 2 claws and release the connector's lock as shown in the illustration.




  1. Detach the claw and disconnect the connector as shown in the illustration.

  1. Remove the driver side junction block assembly.



15. REMOVE MULTIPLEX NETWORK BODY ECU



  1. Press the claw of the junction block as shown in the illustration to release the lock.
    Text in Illustration 
    *1
    		Multiplex Network Body ECU
    *2
    		Driver Side Junction Block
    *3
    		Protective Tape

  1. With the junction block lock released, insert a screwdriver with its tip wrapped with protective tape horizontally between the multiplex network body ECU and junction block.

    NOTICE:
    Use a screwdriver with a radius of between 5.0 mm (0.197 in.) and 6.3 mm (0.248 in.) and a length of approximately 90 mm (3.54 in.).




  1. Using the screwdriver, carefully raise the multiplex network body ECU until the connector becomes disengaged.

    NOTICE:
    Do not twist the screwdriver to raise the multiplex network body ECU.




  1. Raise the multiplex network body ECU as shown by arrow (1), and then pull it out as shown by arrow (2) in the illustration.

    NOTICE:
    Do not touch the ECU connector.
